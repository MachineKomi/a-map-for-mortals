## Page 5 — money · RE-ADJUDICATED against verified primary sources
**Method:** lead + primary-source verification (the Frazier episode taught the lesson: do not
trust the research report's summary). Sources fetched 2026-07-07:
- **Killingsworth, Kahneman & Mellers 2023** (adversarial collaboration) — via u-0026/u-0027 (verified-secondary).
- **Rohrer & Wenz 2024, PNAS** (doi:10.1073/pnas.2313712121), title verbatim from OpenAlex:
  *"Inappropriate causal assumptions underlie Killingsworth, Kahneman, and Mellers' conclusions."*
  Abstract (verbatim): "all assessed quantiles of happiness increase with log(income) until
  $100,000. Beyond $100K, lower quantiles do not increase significantly with log(income),
  suggesting a widening happiness distribution."
- **Bennedsen 2024, Economics Letters** (doi:10.1016/j.econlet.2024.111730), title verbatim:
  *"Income and emotional well-being: Evidence for well-being plateauing around $200,000 per year."*

**Three errors found and corrected (round-3 P0-7):**
1. **Quantile presented as a subgroup of people.** The label "unhappiest ~15–20%: flattens" and
   a-0024's "unhappy minority" read a *conditional-quantile* result as a class of unhappy
   individuals. Rohrer & Wenz's whole point is that this identification needs causal/rank
   assumptions the data do not supply. Fixed: labels and a-0024 now use "lower conditional
   quantiles" and state the distribution-widening reading.
2. **One-sided threshold.** The chart fixed a ~$100k band and drew only the 2023 reading.
   a-0024 now names Bennedsen's ~$200k alternative; the caption states the drawn band is one
   model among competing ones. *(Owed: render the competing model shape / move to a comparison
   form — a chart-form change, queued with the empirical-dossier object.)*
3. **Construct overreach.** a-0025 said "the old claim survives" — but income↔well-being cannot
   test Schopenhauer's comparative claim that *what one is* matters far more than *what one has*
   (the studies do not weigh character/temperament/relationships against money). Removed; a-0025
   now states only what the data settle (a strong "money doesn't matter" reading fails) and what
   they leave untested.

Title changed "What money does — a sketch of the findings" → association language.
