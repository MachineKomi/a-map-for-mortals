# Intellectual-History Layer — *A Map for Mortals*

> **Purpose (METHODOLOGY §4 / §9).** When two claims from different traditions express similar wisdom, the question is whether that is INDEPENDENT convergence (the insight was worked out twice) or TRANSMISSION (one inherited it from the other). This file is the documented-transmission record that `CONVERGES_WITH` independence grading argues against. It is the PRIMARY method for grading independence; text-reuse tooling (Passim/Tesserae, §6) is optional and candidate-only.
>
> **Honesty contract (METHODOLOGY §7, §9).** Independence is arguable, never provable. Where transmission is known or probable we record it; where no channel is known we write *"no known transmission channel"* — **absence of evidence of transmission is not evidence of independence.** Every entry cites the corpus source (report §, edge, unit note) that records it. This is a **first cut** (2026-07-17); it will deepen as depth passes (Epictetus, Analects, Gita, Montaigne) and corpus-wide S4 run.

## 1 · Documented direct transmission (high confidence)

Where we KNOW one thinker read another, or a named channel carries a text forward. Such convergence is **transmission, not independence** — book as `INFLUENCED`, never as independent recurrence on a convergence map.

**The Greek tragedian → Nietzsche chain.** Nietzsche was a philologist; *The Birth of Tragedy* is an explicit reading of Aeschylus and Sophocles. His endorsement of *pathei mathos* (suffering teaches/strengthens) is transmission, not independent rediscovery. Source: edge **e-0006** (c-0023 Aeschylus → c-0008 Nietzsche), retyped from CONVERGES_WITH at the 2026-07-07 adversarial pass because "transmission near-certain (Nietzsche the philologist; *The Birth of Tragedy*)". The adjudication record warns booking this as convergence "would inflate 'tested across time' for the suffering fork." Adjudication: `ops/adjudications/2026-07-07-s3s4-skeleton.md`, DIVERGE item 8.

**Seneca → Pascal (via Montaigne).** Pascal's deferred-living motif depends on Seneca's wasted-life material, mediated by Montaigne. Source: edge **e-0005** (c-0003 → c-0015), confidence moderate, origin `inferred-then-curated`. The mechanism difference (busyness vs anticipation) is retained as a real REFINES, not a collapse. Adjudication DIVERGE item 7.

**Seneca / Renaissance Neo-Stoicism → Shakespeare (*Lear*).** "Renaissance Neo-Stoicism" is a NAMED transmission channel: Senecan drama and the Stoic letters → Justus Lipsius → Elizabethan/Jacobean England; Florio's 1603 Montaigne sits in this channel. Shakespeare's *King Lear* inherits Stoic acceptance. Source: edge **e-0003** (c-0001 Stoic acceptance → c-0021 "ripeness is all"), retyped from CONVERGES_WITH because "Renaissance Neo-Stoicism (Senecan drama; Florio's 1603 Montaigne) makes transmission likely." The play still COMPLICATES the claim — show as lineage on the convergence map, never as independent recurrence. Adjudication DIVERGE item 6. (Note: the line is Edgar's, not Lear's — report 07 watchlist; the speaker attribution does not affect the transmission finding.)

**The Socrates → Plato → Aristotle lineage.** Direct teacher-student transmission within the Academy; report 02 carries these as standard. Aristotle's ethics read against Plato's dialogues is transmission by definition. Cicero transmits Plato explicitly — report 02 unit note on the "philosopher's life is a preparation for death" line: "Cicero is here transmitting Plato (*Phaedo* 64a–67e). Credit both."

**Epictetus → Marcus Aurelius (Stoic lineage).** Marcus read and repeatedly quotes Epictetus (via Arrian's *Discourses*); the *Meditations* are an inner application of Epictetan doctrine (dichotomy of control, prohairesis). Report 02 treats the Greek Stoic corpus as a single transmission stream (Carter 1758, Long 1862 translations; Epictetus "via Arrian"). This is the load-bearing internal lineage for the corpus's largest cluster (the Stoic control/acceptance cluster that feeds skeleton claim c-0001).

**Schopenhauer ← Upanishads / Buddhism.** Schopenhauer engaged deeply with the Upanishads (via Anquetil-Duperron's Latin *Oupnek'hat*) and with Buddhist thought as he could access it; his Eastern-adjacent claims (will-as-thing-in-itself, denial-of-the-will, the porcupine distance parable) are partly transmitted. Report 06 carries Schopenhauer as a modern-West unit; report wave-2/01 notes its §E candidates must "flag convergences instead" of treating Schopenhauer↔Buddhist parallels as independent (COVERAGE-INDEX §4 row "Happiness comes from within…· Schopenhauer/Emerson"). Grade any Schopenhauer↔Eastern convergence as transmission-weighted, not independent.

**Harari ← Vipassana / Buddhist framings.** Harari is a practicing Vipassana meditator and cites Buddhist framings in *21 Lessons*; his "hold your story lightly" / "clarity is power" material draws on this practice. Report wave-3/01 §E item 1: "Harari borrows from Vipassana, so influence is plausible" for the Harari↔Stoic/Buddhist non-attachment convergence (bh-014, bh-012). The convergence-to-test is explicitly routed as a hypothesis to test for "genuine continuity vs surface analogy," not asserted.

**Burkeman ← the ancients (Seneca) and Heidegger.** Burkeman cites the ancients directly; *Four Thousand Weeks* draws on Heidegger's "being-towards-death" (report wave-3/03 units U14, U17 note "invokes Heidegger"). His finitude/cosmic-insignificance material is a secular restatement that travels a known channel. Report wave-3/03 §E item 1: convergence with the ancient carpe-diem/memento-mori cluster is "TO BE TESTED … note Burkeman himself cites the ancients." Grade as transmission-weighted.

**Frankl ← Stoic sources (and Nietzsche).** Frankl's "choose your attitude toward unavoidable suffering" sits in explicit continuity with the Stoic dichotomy of control — report wave-3/04 §E item 1 flags this as a convergence-to-test with a noted disanalogy (Stoic detachment vs Frankl's positive meaning-engagement). Frankl also quotes/adapts Nietzsche's "he who has a why…" (report wave-3/04 MW-01: the line is Nietzsche's, *Twilight of the Idols*, that Frankl deploys). Transmission-weighted.

**Becker ← Kierkegaard (and Rank/Freud).** *The Denial of Death* is a synthetic psychodynamic framework explicitly drawing on Kierkegaard, Rank, and Freud (report wave-3/04 SM-06 source note). Becker's mortality-denial thesis is transmission from these precursors, not independent of them.

**Appiah ← classical cosmopolitanism.** Appiah engages Stoic *oikeiosis* and Diogenes's "citizen of the world" explicitly in *Cosmopolitanism*; report wave-3/04 §E item 3 flags the shared root of universal concern, with a disanalogy (Appiah rejects the "icy impartiality," defends partiality). Transmission-weighted: the modern book knowingly inherits the ancient concept.

**Singer's "expanding circle" ← Lecky 1869.** The phrase and image are W. E. H. Lecky's (*History of European Morals*, 1869), not Singer's — report wave-3/04 SM-28 + MW-03. Singer explicitly credits and adapts Lecky (echoed via Darwin). Any "Singer invented the expanding circle" framing is a misattribution; the concept is transmitted.

**The Panchatantra → Europe chain (paradigm case for proverb diffusion).** Composed in Sanskrit (~300 BCE–300 CE) → Middle Persian/Pahlavi by Burzōy (~570 CE) → Arabic *Kalīla wa-Dimna* by Ibn al-Muqaffaʿ (~750 CE) → Old Spanish/Hebrew/Latin via al-Andalus → all of Europe, reaching La Fontaine (who credited "Pilpay"/Bidpai). Report wave-2/08 §E grades the "don't count unhatched gains" convergence (ATU 1430, the milkmaid's daydream) **likely-transmitted** on this documented chain. **Default: Indian–European proverb parallels are likely transmitted, not independent**, absent evidence of no contact.

**Thoreau ↔ the French moralists (incl. La Rochefoucauld) — FLAGGED, not yet corpus-attested.** The known-historical channel is that Thoreau read the French moralists (La Rochefoucauld, La Bruyère, Vauvenargues) at second hand, which would make any Thoreau↔moralist psychological convergence transmission-weighted. **However: this channel is NOT recorded in the corpus reports.** Report 06 holds Thoreau (Walden units) and La Rochefoucauld (Maximes units) as separate thinkers and never asserts the reading relationship. Record here as a candidate channel pending a depth pass / primary-source check; do not grade any Thoreau↔French-moralist convergence as transmitted-or-independent until a corpus source records it. (This entry exists to model the discipline: a known-to-the-editor channel is still flagged "unattested" when the corpus does not yet support it.)

**Amenemope → Proverbs; Neoplatonism → Augustine and Sufism.** Report 05 flags these lineage channels explicitly in its header: "lineage/cross-pollination is flagged where known (Amenemope→Proverbs; Hellenism→Qoheleth; Neoplatonism→Augustine and Sufism)." CONV-5 (al-Ghazali "he who knows himself knows God" ↔ Augustine's interior turn) is graded "cross-pollination, NOT independent convergence — both draw on a shared Neoplatonic (Plotinus) lineage."

## 2 · Probable / indirect transmission (moderate confidence)

Shared milieu or likely-but-undocumented influence. Grade convergence here as `independence_basis: partial` or `weak` — the channel is plausible but not pinned to a specific text read.

**Hellenistic influence on tannaitic / rabbinic thought.** Report 05 header flags "Hellenism→Qoheleth"; CONV-1 (Ecclesiastes 2:24 "eat, drink, find joy" ↔ Gilgamesh/Siduri) is graded "convergence is partly NOT independent … Qoheleth shows probable Mesopotamian/Hellenistic influence and the motif circulates widely in ANE wisdom. Robustness: moderate, lineage-suspect." General Hellenistic-rabbinic contact is documented; whether it carries a *specific* saying is not.

**Mesopotamian ↔ Hebrew wisdom (shared Fertile-Crescent milieu).** CONV-2 (Job + Psalm 73 ↔ *Ludlul bēl nēmeqi* + Babylonian Theodicy): "2 cultures (Hebrew + Mesopotamian), spanning ~5–10 centuries, with plausible cultural contact along the Fertile Crescent (both Lambert and Kramer note the Mesopotamian texts predate Job). Robustness: high as a recurring human question; independence only partial (shared regional milieu)." High robustness as a recurring question; only partial independence.

**Stoic diffusion in the late-antique Near East (possible for the hadith).** Report wave-2/08 §E grades the "tie your camel" (Tirmidhī) ↔ "pray but lend a hand" (Hippocrates) ↔ "row away from the rocks" convergence as **plausibly-independent (tentative)**: "Greek medical + Arabic religious sources; different vehicles (camel/rowing) — but Mediterranean contact keeps the grade tentative." The channel is plausible; different metaphorical vehicles partially count against copying.

**Confucian ↔ Daoist mutual influence in classical China.** The two traditions co-developed in the same Warring-States milieu and polemicize against each other throughout (report wave-2/01); the Analects and the *Daodejing* are in dialogue. Treat Confucian↔Daoist convergences as milieu-shared, not cleanly independent.

**The didactic-instruction genre as a transmission confound.** Report 05 CONV-4 and COVERAGE-INDEX §4 warn that "shared didactic genre inflates apparent convergence" — the Socratic / Ptahhotep / Proverbs / à Kempis / Euripides humility cluster looks convergent partly because the instruction-text genre carries common forms across the ANE and Mediterranean. Genre is a soft transmission channel.

**Schopenhauer's Buddhism received at second-hand (the mediation problem).** Schopenhauer's access to Buddhism was filtered through early Orientalist scholarship; some of what he "transmitted" was itself a partial rendering. This makes his Eastern-adjacent claims transmission-weighted but with a fuzzy channel — useful to record so we do not over-claim either transmission or the independence of later thinkers who cite Schopenhauer.

## 3 · Plausibly independent (the convergence candidates)

Where similar wisdom appears in traditions with **no known transmission channel**. These are the corpus's strongest independence candidates. **Never mark "proven independent" — only "no known transmission channel."** Grades below cite the corpus source and the residual uncertainty.

**Ubuntu (Nguni) ↔ Golden Rule / one-body solidarity (Hillel, Sermon on the Mount, Saadi).** Report 05 CONV-3 grades this "among the most genuinely independent convergences here … 3 traditions, little direct contact between Saadi (13th-c. Persia) and Hillel (1st-c. Judea), ~13 centuries apart. Robustness: high." Report wave-2/02 flags the Ubuntu↔Golden-Rule/one-body cluster for merge (COVERAGE-INDEX §7). Residual caveat: the Golden Rule is so general it may recur from common human psychology; report wave-2/08 grades proverbial convergences that depend on universal experience as "plausibly independent (universal experience) but" with cross-pollination possible. **Grade: no known channel — strongest independence candidate in the corpus.**

**Indigenous seventh-generation / stewardship-as-relationship ↔ covenantal stewardship (and Diamond/Christian stewardship).** Report wave-2/03 §E: "Reciprocity/relationality: Andean *ayni* ↔ Sámi *láhi* ↔ Potawatomi Honorable Harvest ↔ Lakota *Mitákuye Oyás'iŋ* ↔ African ubuntu — Test: genuine independent convergence, or analytic over-generalization imposed by outsiders? (Recurrence ≠ proof.)" Report wave-3/01 §E item 5 flags Diamond/Christian stewardship ↔ "intergenerational-obligation wisdom ('seventh-generation,' covenantal stewardship)" as a convergence to test. **Caveat (critical):** the popular "in every deliberation consider the seventh generation" is NOT a verbatim Great Law quotation (report wave-2/03); base any convergence on the authentic Haudenosaunee "faces yet beneath the ground" ethic, not the modern slogan. Indigenous-to-Abrahamic stewardship parallels have no documented channel but the test for "outsider-imposed over-generalization" is required before grading. **Grade: no known channel, but require ≥3 peoples + an Indigenous scholar per tradition before upgrading (report wave-2/03 recommendation).**

**Buddhist *anattā* (non-self) ↔ Humean bundle-self.** Both dissolve a reified, enduring self. Report wave-2/01 carries the anattā / Nagarjuna *śūnyatā* material; report wave-3/04 §E item 4 flags "Buddhist non-self (*anattā*) ↔ Appiah's identity-as-fiction" as a convergence to test (with a noted disanalogy: Buddhism is metaphysical/soteriological; Appiah is socio-historical about group labels). The Hume side is unsourced in the corpus until a depth pass — report 06 holds Hume (*Treatise*, the "is-ought" line) but the bundle-self is not yet a unit. **Grade: no known channel between Hume and the Buddhist scholastics (Hume did not read Pali sources; contact is via later Orientalist reception) — but flag that the comparison is currently one-sided pending the Hume unit.**

**Mohist impartial care (*jiān'ài*) ↔ Singer's impartial beneficence.** Report wave-3/04 §E item 5: "structurally similar rejections of graded, kin-first concern. Disanalogy: Mohism grounds impartial care in Heaven's will and social order; Singer in secular preference-utilitarian consequences." Mohism (China, c. 4th c. BCE) and Singer (20th-c. Australia) have no documented channel; the structural similarity with divergent grounding is a strong independence profile. **Grade: no known channel — strong independence candidate, cross-ref classical Chinese cluster.**

**Tao Te Ching gradualism (ch. 64) ↔ Tortoise-and-Hare (Greek).** Report wave-2/08 §E grades the slow-and-steady convergence **plausibly-independent** between Tao and Greek forms: "Chinese and Greek forms have no documented contact; distinct images (journey/tower vs. race)." (Within Greek→French it is transmitted.) **Grade: no known channel for the Tao↔Greek pole.**

**Seneca's imagined-suffering ↔ Buddhist *papañca* (hypothetical to test).** COVERAGE-INDEX §4: "Imagined suffering exceeds real suffering | 06 (Seneca via pseudo-Montaigne), Buddhist parallel named … Buddhist side unsourced until Eastern report lands." ## 4 · Translation / reception events (language-boundary crossings)

The moments a text crossed a language boundary. These matter because they re-route transmission: a tradition "influencing" another often did so only once a translation existed, sometimes centuries after composition.

**Aristotle via Arabic commentary back to Latin.** The classical Aristotelian corpus survived into the Latin West largely through Syriac → Arabic translation and commentary (Avicenna/Ibn Sīnā, Averroes/Ibn Rushd), then back-translated into Latin (Toledo, 12th c.). This is why medieval Scholasticism (Aquinas) read an Arabic-mediated Aristotle. Relevant to Appiah's "no such thing as Western civilization" point (report wave-3/04 SM-25): "classical learning was preserved and transmitted through the Muslim world." Any Aristotle→Latin-Christian convergence routes through this event.

**Buddhist sutras: Sanskrit → Chinese → English.** The Mahāyāna corpus crossed from Sanskrit into Chinese (Kumārajīva, 4th–5th c.; Xuanzang, 7th c.) and much of what the English-speaking West reads is re-translated from the Chinese, not the Sanskrit originals, with some originals since lost. Report wave-2/01 flags that Nagarjuna *MMK* and Dōgen *Genjōkōan* verses are "probable" pending check against Garfield (Oxford 1995, from Tibetan) and Tanahashi editions. **Translation layer = a real interpretive variable; do not assume the English wording is the original claim.**

**The *Rubáiyát* via FitzGerald.** Report wave-2/06 treats FitzGerald as the discipline gold-standard: all Rubaiyat quatrains are dubious / pseudepigraphy dated; FitzGerald's 1859 "translation" is substantially a creative re-rendering. Khayyam reception in English is a FitzGerald event, not direct Khayyam transmission. Any Omar Khayyam unit carries this caveat.

**The Upanishads via Anquetil-Duperron's *Oupnek'hat* (Latin, from Persian).** This is the specific channel Schopenhauer read — Sanskrit → Persian → Latin → German. Each step is a rendering; Schopenhauer's "Eastern" material is twice-mediated. Records as the mechanism behind §1's Schopenhauer←Upanishads entry.

**Florio's Montaigne (1603).** The English reception of Montaigne — and through him, of Seneca and the Stoics — is via John Florio's 1603 translation; this is the named channel in edge e-0003 (Renaissance Neo-Stoicism → *Lear*). Shakespeare and his contemporaries met Montaigne/Seneca in Florio's English, not the French or Latin.

**Hafiz / Rumi pseudo-quotation ecosystem (a negative reception event).** Report 05 warns this is "the single largest data-integrity hazard for ingestion"; report wave-2/06 quarantines it. The English-language "Rumi" and "Hafiz" quotation corpus is substantially a modern Western reception phenomenon (Barks etc.), not faithful transmission. Treat the popular English Rumi/Hafiz material as a reception artifact, not a Persian-wisdom channel.

## Honesty contract (binding)

1. **Cite the corpus source** for every transmission claim above (report §, edge ID, unit note, adjudication record). Where this file states "documented," a named edge or report records it; where "probable," it is flagged as such.
2. **Never claim independence is PROVEN.** Section 3 says "no known transmission channel," never "proven independent" (METHODOLOGY §7 independence checklist: absence of evidence of transmission ≠ evidence of absence). Even Ubuntu↔Golden-Rule is "strongest independence candidate," not "proven."
3. **Flag uncertainty explicitly.** Confidence follows the grading of the source: e-0003/e-0005/e-0006 carry `confidence: moderate` and `origin: inferred-then-curated`; report-wave-2/08 grades carry the `plausibly-independent | likely-transmitted | undetermined` enum; report 05 convergences carry robustness + lineage-suspect notes.
4. **This is a FIRST CUT (2026-07-17).** It will deepen as: (a) depth passes land (Epictetus *Discourses*, Montaigne essay-by-essay, Analects/Gita book-by-book — COVERAGE-INDEX §3 P2); (b) corpus-wide S4 runs and grades every `CONVERGES_WITH` against this record; (c) primary-edition verification clears the "probable" Nagarjuna/Dōgen units (report wave-2/01); (d) the Mohism and Hume-bundle units fill out the one-sided independence candidates in §3; (e) un-attested-in-corpus channels (e.g., the Thoreau↔French-moralists reading relationship) are either confirmed by a corpus source or struck.
5. **The map's discipline.** When the book renders a convergence map, transmitted voices appear as **lineage (dashed, labelled)**, never as independent recurrence — the retyped INFLUENCED edges (e-0003, e-0005, e-0006) exist precisely so "this idea travelled" is a different fact from "this idea was rediscovered" (adjudication record, "Consequence for the render layer"). This file is the evidence base that decides which is which.
