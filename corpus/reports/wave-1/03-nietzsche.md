# A Map for Mortals — The Nietzsche Corpus & His Approved Precursors
### A graph-ingestible, source-critical research report

## TL;DR
- **Nietzsche genuinely admired a tight, verifiable constellation of thinkers** — Heraclitus and the Pre-Socratics, Thucydides, Goethe, Dostoevsky, Stendhal, Montaigne, Emerson, Spinoza, the French moralists (La Rochefoucauld, Chamfort), and Heine — and for each the praise can be tied to a specific work, section, and (often) a verbatim quote. Socrates/Plato, Schopenhauer, and early Wagner are *ambivalent* and admitted only in the dimensions Nietzsche actually endorsed.
- **The two flagged quotes check out**: "What does not kill me makes me stronger" is VERIFIED (*Twilight of the Idols*, "Maxims and Arrows" [The Marginalian](https://www.themarginalian.org/2015/09/18/nietzsche-on-music/) §8); "he who has a why to live can bear almost any how" is a GENUINE idea (*Twilight*, "Maxims and Arrows" §12) but the popular wording is Viktor Frankl's paraphrase, which Frankl explicitly credited to Nietzsche. A large volume of viral "Nietzsche" lines are fabricated or misattributed.
- **"The Will to Power" is not a Nietzsche book.** It is a posthumous compilation assembled by his sister Elisabeth Förster-Nietzsche with Peter Gast (Heinrich Köselitz); editors Colli and Montinari called it a "historic forgery." Use only individual authenticated Nachlass notes, cautiously, and never as doctrinal proof of a metaphysical "will to power."

---

## Key Findings
The corpus splits cleanly into (1) Nietzsche's own published works, all available in public-domain English translations, and (2) a small circle of approved precursors whose endorsement is textually documented. Kaufmann and Hollingdale remain the scholarly-standard translations but are under copyright; the public-domain alternatives (Thomas Common, Helen Zimmern, Horace B. Samuel, Anthony M. Ludovici, William A. Haussmann, the Oscar Levy *Complete Works* of 1909–1913) are adequate substitutes with occasionally meaningful wording differences that must be flagged. The biggest source-critical hazards are (a) the fabricated-quote ecosystem online and (b) the editorial distortions of the Förster-Nietzsche archive.

## CENTRAL FORKS NIETZSCHE POSES (choices under tension)

1. **Life-affirmation vs. life-denial (Dionysian Yes vs. ascetic No).** Affirm existence including its suffering, or seek a "true world" that devalues this one? → vitality and creativity vs. ressentiment and decadence.
2. **Self-overcoming vs. self-preservation.** Grow by overcoming yourself, or merely conserve and protect the self? (His critique of Spinoza's conatus lives here.)
3. **Noble/master valuation vs. herd/slave valuation.** Do values flow from strength and self-affirmation, or from reactive resentment of the strong?
4. **Intellectual honesty vs. comforting illusion.** Pursue truth even when it wounds, or prefer consoling fictions?
5. **Solitude vs. the herd.** Bear the loneliness of "becoming who you are," or seek the warmth of conformity?
6. **Amor fati vs. revolt against the necessary.** Love what is necessary, or wage war on the ugly and unchosen?

## VIRTUES EXALTED / DANGERS NAMED
- **Virtues exalted:** intellectual honesty (*Redlichkeit*); courage in the face of reality; self-overcoming; productive solitude; the "gift-giving virtue"; cheerfulness (*Heiterkeit*); discipline of one's contradictions into wholeness.
- **Dangers named:** *ressentiment*; pity (*Mitleid*) as life-draining; the ascetic ideal turned against life; herd conformity; the "spirit of gravity"; nihilism after the death of God; the craving for a "true world" behind appearances.

## CANDIDATE CONVERGENCES & TENSIONS (convergence = robustness, NOT truth)
- **Heraclitus ↔ Nietzsche (convergence):** becoming over being; strife as generative. Nietzsche names Heraclitus the nearest of all prior thinkers to himself.
- **Spinoza ↔ Nietzsche (convergence + tension):** shared denial of teleology, free will, and a moral world-order; both naturalize ethics. **Tension:** Spinozan self-preservation (conatus) vs. Nietzschean self-overcoming/expansion.
- **Montaigne / La Rochefoucauld ↔ Nietzsche (convergence):** undeceived psychology of motives; honesty experienced as joy.
- **Thucydides ↔ Nietzsche (convergence):** realism, anti-Platonism, courage before reality.
- **Dostoevsky ↔ Nietzsche (tension within convergence):** shared psychological depth; opposite conclusions on Christianity and pity.
- **Stoics ↔ Nietzsche (partial convergence):** amor fati echoes Stoic acceptance and the Stoic inheritance from Heraclitus, but Nietzsche rejects Stoic *apatheia* and "living according to nature" as he reads it (*Beyond Good and Evil* §9).

## ELISABETH FÖRSTER-NIETZSCHE / "WILL TO POWER" CAVEAT
After Nietzsche's 1889 collapse and 1900 death, his sister **Elisabeth Förster-Nietzsche** — a committed antisemite (widow of the *völkisch* agitator Bernhard Förster) [Encyclopedia Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/biography/Elisabeth-Forster-Nietzsche) and later a Nazi sympathizer — became his literary executor and head of the Nietzsche-Archiv in Weimar. She compiled *The Will to Power* **together with Peter Gast (Heinrich Köselitz)**: a first 1901 edition and an expanded **1906 edition [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/6114721) of 1,067 sections**, arranged in an order Nietzsche never authorized. **Nietzsche had abandoned the "Will to Power" book plan by late August 1888**, replacing it with "Versuch einer Umwerthung aller Werthe" [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Will_to_Power_(manuscript)) (Attempt at a Revaluation of All Values), which would have ordered the fragments entirely differently. [Fnietzsche](https://fnietzsche.com/the-will-to-power-manuscript-colli-and-montinari-research) Editors **Giorgio Colli and Mazzino Montinari** — who produced the critical edition of the Nachlass — called the book a **"historic forgery"**; [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Will_to_Power_(manuscript)) [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/6114721) Montinari's summary, as transmitted via Wikipedia, is that it "was not a text completed by Nietzsche, but rather an anthology of selections from his notebooks misrepresented as if it were something more." [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Will_to_Power_(manuscript)) [Fnietzsche](https://fnietzsche.com/the-will-to-power-manuscript-colli-and-montinari-research)

The archive's Nazi entanglement is documented: from May 1934 the Nietzsche-Archiv received a monthly stipend of **300 reichsmarks from Hitler's private funds**, [Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Elisabeth_F%C3%B6rster-Nietzsche) and when Elisabeth died on **8 November 1935** in Weimar, **Hitler attended her funeral** [London Review of Books](https://www.lrb.co.uk/the-paper/v25/n18/jenny-diski/it-wasn-t-him-it-was-her) as an honorary guest, where she was eulogized as the "priestess of eternal Germany" [London Review of Books](https://www.lrb.co.uk/the-paper/v25/n18/jenny-diski/it-wasn-t-him-it-was-her) (Jenny Diski, "It wasn't him, it was her," *London Review of Books* 25:18, 2003). Nietzsche himself was explicitly **anti-nationalist and anti-antisemitic** [Audiala](https://audiala.com/en/germany/weimar/nietzsche-archiv) (Elisabeth even deleted passages, such as one in which he laments any association with antisemitism, [Red Sails](https://redsails.org/elisabeths-nietzsche/) and removed §35 of *The Antichrist*). [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friedrich_Nietzsche) Twentieth-century scholars (Kaufmann, Hollingdale, Montinari) corrected the record. **Practical rule for the graph:** treat individual authenticated Nachlass notes as genuine artifacts of Nietzsche's thinking (useful for tracing conceptual evolution), but never cite the *Will to Power* compilation as a finished doctrinal source, [Grokipedia](https://grokipedia.com/page/The_Will_to_Power_(manuscript)) and tag every such unit `source: nachlass-compilation; authorial_endorsement: none`.

## Caveats
- **Recurrence ≠ proof; convergence = robustness, not truth.** That Heraclitus, Spinoza, Montaigne, and Nietzsche converge on naturalism and anti-teleology makes the position *robust*, not *true* — encode it as corroboration, never as proof.
- **Preserve contradiction.** Do not resolve Nietzsche's simultaneous admiration and critique of Spinoza (precursor vs. conatus-critic), Schopenhauer (honest exemplar vs. nihilist), or Socrates (courageous vs. decadent-rationalist). Represent each as a live tension.
- **Translation matters.** Public-domain renderings (Common, Ludovici, Zimmern, Samuel) diverge meaningfully from the copyrighted Kaufmann/Hollingdale standards — e.g., Heine "most perfect idea" (Ludovici) vs. "highest conception" (Kaufmann); the chapter title "Skirmishes in a War with the Age" (Ludovici) vs. "Skirmishes of an Untimely Man" (Kaufmann/Hollingdale). Always credit the translator and edition on every unit, and store the German where the wording is philosophically load-bearing.
- **The "blond beast" and "will to power" are the most-distorted terms.** When ingested, attach interpretive flags: "will to power" handled as a psychological/physiological drive theory, NOT crude political domination; and the *Genealogy* "blond beast" (1st essay §11) read in context, not through the Förster-Nietzsche/Nazi appropriation.
