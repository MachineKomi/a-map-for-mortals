# A Map for Mortals: Research-Methodology Specification

## TL;DR
- The project's committed spec is fundamentally sound and should be reinforced, not rebuilt: public-domain-first sourcing, dual storage (quotation + canonical claim), LLM-proposes/human-verifies unitization, multi-factor robustness profiling, and the Six Degrees of Francis Bacon "inferred-vs-curated" edge model are all well-supported by the literature. The highest-leverage improvements are (1) adopting CTS-URN-style canonical citation and W3C PROV provenance as first-class schema; (2) treating the convergence-vs-independence problem as fundamentally undecidable computationally and labelling it honestly rather than scoring it; and (3) designing the robustness profile as a GRADE-style per-dimension profile that NEVER aggregates to one number.
- For edge construction, NLI models (entailment/contradiction) are usable but have documented failure modes (negation insensitivity, lexical-overlap shortcuts) and must be human-adjudicated; text-reuse tools (Passim, Tesserae, TRACER) can flag probable transmission/influence, but "independent invention" can only ever be argued, never proven — so independence must be a graded, evidence-tagged human judgment, not a model output.
- For robustness, four independent methodological literatures (Saltelli's uncertainty/sensitivity-analysis school, Munda's non-compensatory MCDA, the Bruggemann–Patil–Fattore partial-order/POSET school, and the GRADE evidence-grading standard) converge on one conclusion: collapsing multi-dimensional information into a single score hides incommensurability, smuggles in arbitrary weights that act as hidden trade-offs, and permits undesirable compensation. The profile should stay disaggregated, be presented as a GRADE-style table plus a partial order, and be subjected to sensitivity analysis.

## Key Findings

1. **The existing ontology and five-phase methodology are well-aligned with best practice.** The project should confirm and extend its draft rather than reinvent it. The main additions recommended below are standards-based (CTS/CITE URNs, TEI, PROV-O) and epistemic (independence as graded judgment, non-aggregating robustness profile).

2. **Citation and provenance should be standards-based.** Perseus/Open Greek and Latin use Canonical Text Services (CTS) URNs (e.g. `urn:cts:greekLit:tlg0012.tlg001.perseus-grc2:1.1-1.7`) built on an FRBR-like work/version hierarchy, which solves precisely the "same source, multiple translations/editions" problem the project faces. W3C PROV-O, nanopublications, and Wikidata's statement-rank model are the mature options for distinguishing inferred from curated assertions.

3. **Inter-rater reliability should be reported with Krippendorff's α, not Cohen's κ.** α handles multiple coders, ordinal/nominal scales, and missing data; κ is limited to two coders, fixed categories, no missing data. This matters because the project will have several coders and ordinal fields.

4. **LLM-assisted annotation is validated but must be human-anchored.** Gilardi, Alizadeh & Kubli (2023, PNAS) found "the zero-shot accuracy of ChatGPT exceeds that of crowd workers by about 25 percentage points on average, while ChatGPT's intercoder agreement exceeds that of both crowd workers and trained annotators for all tasks... the per-annotation cost of ChatGPT is less than $0.003—about thirty times cheaper than MTurk." But the strong methodological consensus (e.g. "Automated Annotation with Generative AI Requires Validation") is that LLM labels require validation against a human-coded gold standard with reported agreement.

5. **Embedding model choice should be MTEB-informed but locally validated.** Strong 2024–2025 open-weight options include the BGE family (BGE-M3), E5-Mistral, GTE-Qwen2, and the Qwen3-Embedding family. Generic leaderboard scores do not guarantee performance on short aphoristic "canonical claims," so benchmark on project data.

6. **HDBSCAN-over-UMAP (as in BERTopic) is the right clustering family for a few-thousand-item aphorism space**, because it does not force every item into a cluster (it isolates noise/outliers) and does not require a preset cluster count — but UMAP is stochastic and must be seeded for reproducibility.

7. **NLI for contradiction/entailment has known, documented failure modes** — negation insensitivity, reliance on lexical-overlap shortcuts (the "semantic collapse" problem), and poor performance on subtle cross-document contradictions even for GPT-4-class models. NLI outputs are candidate generators requiring human adjudication, not edge oracles.

8. **Independent convergence cannot be proven computationally.** Text-reuse/intertextuality tools (Passim, Tesserae, TRACER, TextPAIR) and stemmatic/phylogenetic methods can establish *probable transmission*; they cannot establish *independent invention*. Independence is the absence of detectable transmission, which is an argument from silence — so the project must tag it as a graded human judgment with explicit evidence, never a model verdict.

9. **The robustness profile must not aggregate to a single score.** This is supported by convergent critiques from Saltelli's group (composite indicators "can send misleading policy messages"), Munda's non-compensatory MCDA, the POSET/partial-order school, and GRADE.

## Details

### Phase A — Sourcing

**Confirm:** the public-domain-first strategy (Project Gutenberg, Internet Archive, Perseus, ctext.org, SuttaCentral, Access to Insight, Sacred-Texts) with SEP for scholarly framing and Wikiquote treated as untrusted is sound and aligns with digital-humanities norms.

**Extend with standards:**
- **Adopt CTS/CITE URNs as the canonical citation backbone.** The Canonical Text Services URN syntax (developed for the Homer Multitext Project, used by Perseus/Perseids and Open Greek and Latin) encodes both the position of a work in an FRBR-like work→version→passage hierarchy *and* the citable passage range in a single technology-independent identifier [Tufts University](https://sites.tufts.edu/perseusupdates/2012/07/17/dighumtec/) (e.g. `urn:cts:greekLit:tlg0012.tlg001.perseus-grc2:1.1-1.7`). This directly solves the "multiple translations/editions of the same source" problem: each translation is a *version* under the same notional *work*, and a canonical claim can point to the work-level URN while each quotation points to a version+passage URN. Where a source has no CTS URN (most non-classical texts), mint a project-local URN-style identifier following the same work/version/passage logic.
- **Encode source text in TEI where feasible**, or at minimum store the bibliographic structure TEI represents (work, edition, translator, editor, date). Perseus's own workflow (Scaife Viewer / ATLAS) shows TEI XML and a CTS data model can coexist with simpler "citation + plain text" import formats [Pubpub](https://perseus.pubpub.org/pub/hea7nhh5/release/1) — the project does not need full TEI markup of every text, only TEI-compatible metadata.
- **Provenance via W3C PROV.** Model every text as a PROV Entity, every extraction/annotation step as a PROV Activity, and every human/LLM actor as a PROV Agent. This makes "where did this unit come from and who/what touched it" queryable and is the W3C-recommended foundation.
- **Licensing:** public-domain status is jurisdiction-dependent (US "publication before 1929"; life+70 in most other jurisdictions); translations carry their own copyright separate from the original, so a public-domain ancient text may have an in-copyright modern translation. Prefer public-domain or openly licensed (CC) translations, and record license per version in the schema.

### Phase B — Unitization

**Confirm:** LLM-proposes / human-verifies-a-calibrated-sample is the right pattern. Store each unit in two forms (original quotation with citation+translator; neutral one-sentence canonical claim).

**The "unit of analysis" problem:** digital-humanities content analysis has no universal segmentation rule; the unit must be defined by the research question. For a wisdom graph, the atomic unit should be the *minimal self-contained prudential/empirical/normative claim*. Recommend a written segmentation codebook with worked examples and edge cases, and report unitization reliability (do two humans segment the same passage into the same units?) — segmentation disagreement is itself a measurable quantity.

### Phase C — Annotation / Coding

**Codebook design:** follow standard content-analysis practice — explicit definitions, decision rules, positive/negative examples, and a calibration round before full coding.

**Inter-rater reliability — use Krippendorff's α as the primary statistic.** The literature is clear on when to use each:
- **Cohen's κ:** two coders, fixed set of categories, no missing data; defines chance agreement via individual coders' marginal distributions (contingencies). Known oddity: can be deceptively low under class imbalance and treats coders as non-interchangeable.
- **Fleiss' κ:** generalizes to many coders but only for nominal categories with complete data.
- **Krippendorff's α:** any number of coders, any measurement level (nominal/ordinal/interval/ratio), tolerates missing data, treats coders as interchangeable (via a coincidence matrix). α = 1 perfect, 0 = chance, negative = systematic disagreement. It is the de facto standard in content analysis and increasingly in NLP.
- **Thresholds:** conventional Krippendorff guidance: α ≥ 0.80 for firm conclusions, 0.667–0.80 for tentative conclusions. For objective tasks expect >0.8; for subjective tasks 0.6+ is often the realistic ceiling. Report α per coding dimension (like GRADE rates per outcome), not one global number.

**LLM-as-annotator (2023–2025 literature):**
- Gilardi, Alizadeh & Kubli (2023, *PNAS* 120(30):e2305016120): across four datasets (n=6,183), "the zero-shot accuracy of ChatGPT exceeds that of crowd workers by about 25 percentage points on average, while ChatGPT's intercoder agreement exceeds that of both crowd workers and trained annotators for all tasks... the per-annotation cost of ChatGPT is less than $0.003—about thirty times cheaper than MTurk."
- Törnberg (2023): GPT-4 outperformed both experts and crowdworkers in annotating political tweets. [Semantic Scholar](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/ChatGPT-4-Outperforms-Experts-and-Crowd-Workers-in-T%C3%B6rnberg/6354f2639d07bf8d5b08a4dcaef4c5db5fe19fdb)
- Counter-evidence and cautions: Reiss (2023) found LLMs perform poorly on some annotation and urges caution; "Automated Annotation with Generative AI Requires Validation" (2023) argues LLM labelling workflows must be validated per dataset/task and that reporting only accuracy/agreement is insufficient.
- **Recommended workflow:** LLM proposes labels with rationale → humans adjudicate a stratified calibration sample → compute human-vs-LLM agreement (Krippendorff's α) per dimension → if α is adequate, let the LLM scale out, with ongoing human spot-checks; if not, the dimension stays human-coded. Always report the LLM model+version, prompt, temperature, and date (reproducibility).

### Phase D — Semantic Structuring

**Embeddings:** select an MTEB-leading sentence-embedding model but validate locally. Open-weight families to benchmark: **BGE-M3** (BAAI; dense+sparse+multi-vector, strong for production retrieval), [atal upadhyay](https://atalupadhyay.wordpress.com/2026/02/13/embedding-models-for-rag-top-5-models-ranked-benchmarked/) **E5-Mistral** (LLM-backbone), **GTE-Qwen2**, and the **Qwen3-Embedding** family (top of recent MTEB multilingual leaderboards, user-defined output dimensions). Commercial options (OpenAI text-embedding-3-large, Cohere Embed, Voyage) are alternatives but trade away reproducibility/transparency. Because the corpus is cross-lingual (Greek, Latin, Chinese, Pali, Sanskrit, English translations), prioritise a strong multilingual model and consider embedding the English canonical claim as the primary comparison field, with original-language embeddings as a secondary check.

**Clustering:** **HDBSCAN over UMAP-reduced embeddings** (the BERTopic pipeline) is well-suited to a few-thousand-item aphorism space: HDBSCAN finds variable-density clusters, requires no preset cluster count, and crucially marks outliers as noise rather than forcing every aphorism into a theme. Caveats: UMAP is stochastic — set a fixed `random_state` for reproducibility; tune `n_neighbors` (local vs global structure) and `min_cluster_size` (topic granularity); use cosine distance for the UMAP step (matches sentence-transformer geometry) and Euclidean on the reduced space for HDBSCAN. Cross-check cluster structure with an alternative (agglomerative clustering, or Leiden/Louvain community detection on a k-NN similarity graph) — agreement across methods is evidence of real structure, not an artifact.

**Near-duplicate / paraphrase detection across texts, translations, and eras:** use the sentence-transformers **paraphrase-mining** utility (all-pairs cosine similarity with chunking) plus a cross-encoder reranker for precision. SimCSE-style contrastive models and dedicated STS models are appropriate. Known failure mode (documented in clinical STS work): transformer STS models tend to **over-predict similarity when there is high token overlap** and under-detect similarity when wording diverges — exactly the cross-translation/cross-era case — so human verification of borderline paraphrase pairs is required.

### Phase E — Edge Construction (research-grade)

This is the project's hardest problem. Decompose it into two distinct tasks that the literature treats very differently.

**(i) Semantic relation typing (convergence / contradiction / refinement-entailment).**
- Use **Natural Language Inference (NLI)** models for entailment/contradiction candidate generation. State-of-the-art models are transformer-based (RoBERTa/DeBERTa/XLM-RoBERTa fine-tuned on SNLI/MNLI/ANLI).
- **Documented failure modes** the project must guard against: (a) negation insensitivity / "negation is not semantic" — dense models lose negation signals when topics are similar; (b) lexical-overlap shortcuts — McCoy et al.'s HANS showed models systematically misclassify high-word-overlap contradictions as agreement; (c) dataset-artifact dependence (SNLI/MNLI shortcuts); (d) even GPT-4-class models miss subtle cross-document contradictions (ContraDoc), and trained human annotators miss many too. Best human-bot contradiction-detection scores on DECODE (~80.9%) still trail humans (~84.7%).
- **Recommendation:** NLI is a candidate-generator, not an oracle. Every CONTRADICTS/IN_TENSION_WITH and REFINES/QUALIFIES edge proposed by NLI must be human-adjudicated. Distinguish *logical* contradiction from *prudential tension* (two pieces of advice that pull in opposite directions under different conditions) — the latter is the project's actual subject matter and is usually NOT a clean NLI "contradiction."

**(ii) Convergence vs transmission/influence — the independence problem.**
- **Establishing transmission/influence (positive detection):** intertextuality/text-reuse pipelines can flag probable borrowing: **Tesserae** (bigram matching by form and lemma, built for Latin poetry), **Passim** (Smith/Cordell/Mullen; Scala; scales from small collections to massive corpora; good for detecting shared passages and multiple editions), **TRACER** (Büchler et al.; highly configurable, multilingual), **TextPAIR** (ARTFL), and word-embedding-based allusion detection. ctext.org has text-reuse tooling for the Chinese classics. These find shared *wording*; they detect transmission, citation, and allusion.
- **The deep epistemic limit:** independence is the *absence* of detectable transmission. Stemmatics (Lachmann's method: shared errors indicate common descent) and computational phylogenetics (Bayesian/maximum-likelihood "descent with modification," PAUP*, applied by Robinson, Howe, Barbrook, etc.) reconstruct *genealogies of descent* — and even there, **contamination** (a scribe using multiple sources) and **polygenesis** (the same change arising independently) are explicitly recognized as confounds that break the tree model. The analogue for ideas is exact: a maxim appearing in two traditions could be (a) transmitted (Silk Road, translation movements, shared scripture), (b) descended from a common ancestor, or (c) independently invented because it tracks a real regularity of human life. **No computational method can distinguish (c) from undocumented (a)/(b)** — this is an argument from silence.
- **Recommendation:** Do NOT compute an "independence score." Instead: (1) run text-reuse detection to find *positive* evidence of transmission and record it as INFLUENCED / FROM_WORK / IN_TRADITION edges, tagged inferred; (2) build an explicit intellectual-history layer (known contact between traditions, translation history, chronology) so that geographic/temporal/linguistic separation can be *argued*; (3) represent independence as a graded, human-assigned `independence_basis` field on convergence relationships (e.g. "no known contact + different language family + chronological priority in both" → higher; "both post-date a known translation event" → lower), always with the evidence attached. Convergence weighted by independence is the project's signal — but it is robustness, not truth, and the weighting must be transparent and revisable.

### Phase F — Robustness Profiling (deepest dive)

**The core decision is correct and should be defended forcefully: do NOT collapse to a single truth score.** Four independent literatures converge:

1. **Saltelli's uncertainty/sensitivity-analysis school.** Saisana, Saltelli & Tarantola (2005, *JRSS-A* 168(2):307–323): "Composite indicators may send misleading, non-robust policy messages if they are poorly constructed or misinterpreted. Sensitivity analysis can be used to test composite indicators for robustness." Paruolo, Saisana & Saltelli (2013, *JRSS-A* 176(3), "Ratings and rankings: Voodoo or science?"): because socio-economic variables are correlated and heteroskedastic, *nominal weights almost never equal actual importance* [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1104.3009) — the "linear aggregation paradox" is that weights are elicited as importance coefficients but mathematically function as trade-off (substitution) coefficients ("relative nominal weights are hardly ever found to match relative main effects"). Saisana, d'Hombres & Saltelli (2011, *Research Policy* 40(1), "Rickety numbers") showed university rankings (THES, Shanghai ARWU) shift dramatically under defensible methodological changes.

2. **The OECD/EU-JRC Handbook on Constructing Composite Indicators (Nardo, Saisana, Saltelli, Tarantola, Hoffmann, Giovannini, 2008; ISBN 978-92-64-04345-9).** Its 10-step framework treats a composite indicator like a model whose construction "owes more to the craftsmanship of the modeller than to universally accepted scientific rules," [OECD](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2008/08/handbook-on-constructing-composite-indicators-methodology-and-user-guide_g1gh9301/9789264043466-en.pdf) warns that "big picture" results "may invite users (especially policy-makers) to draw simplistic analytical or policy conclusions," [OECD](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2008/08/handbook-on-constructing-composite-indicators-methodology-and-user-guide_g1gh9301/9789264043466-en.pdf) and mandates Step 7 (uncertainty + sensitivity analysis via Monte Carlo over indicator inclusion, normalisation, weighting, aggregation), Step 9 ("back to the details" — decompose the index to its components), and Step 10 (present alongside sub-indicators using radar charts, traffic-light tables, etc.). It explicitly notes linear aggregation implies "full (and constant) compensability," geometric mean "partial (non-constant) compensability," and that under these schemes weights are trade-offs, not importance coefficients.

3. **Munda's non-compensatory MCDA.** Munda & Nardo (2009, *Applied Economics* 41(12):1513–1523, "Noncompensatory/nonlinear composite indicators for ranking countries: a defensible setting") and Munda (2012, *Social Indicators Research* 109(3):337–354, "Choosing Aggregation Rules for Composite Indicators"): full compensability (a deficit in one dimension fully offset by a surplus in another at a constant rate) is "often undesirable from a normative perspective." [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S037722171830208X) Munda's alternative is a Condorcet-consistent non-compensatory multi-criteria aggregation [European Commission](https://publications.jrc.ec.europa.eu/repository/bitstream/JRC32435/EUR%2021833%20EN.pdf) when an ordering is genuinely needed, grounded in *Social Multi-Criteria Evaluation* (Munda 2008, Springer).

4. **The Bruggemann–Patil–Fattore partial-order (POSET) school.** Bruggemann & Patil (2011, *Ranking and Prioritization for Multi-Indicator Systems*, Springer): partial-order ranking needs no weights and no aggregation. Two items are comparable only if one dominates on *all* dimensions; [Wiley Online Library](https://analyticalsciencejournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/cem.2569) otherwise they are *incomparable* (an antichain), [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/303650686_From_Composite_Indicators_to_Partial_Orders_Evaluating_Socio-Economic_Phenomena_Through_Ordinal_Data) visualised in a **Hasse diagram** (software: PyHasse). This preserves incommensurability instead of hiding it. Fattore and colleagues extended it to ordinal socio-economic data; Carlsen & Bruggemann applied it to critique the Failed States Index. The school's argument: aggregative approaches "mix-up complex multi-indicator systems into single aggregates, introducing compensations among attributes and, to a large extent, arbitrary weighting that render the final indices somehow artificial."

5. **GRADE** (medical evidence grading; Guyatt, Oxman et al., *J Clin Epidemiol* 2011–2013; Cochrane Handbook Ch. 14): rates certainty **per outcome** (high/moderate/low/very low) across explicit domains (risk of bias, inconsistency, indirectness, imprecision, publication bias) [Elsevierpure](https://ohsu.elsevierpure.com/en/publications/grade-guidelines-1-introduction-grade-evidence-profiles-and-summa-2) and presents results in a **Summary of Findings table** [Journal of Clinical Epidemiology](https://www.jclinepi.com/article/S0895-4356(12)00032-7/fulltext) — deliberately NOT one number. Caps outcomes at ~7 to avoid overload; [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0895435612000327) pairs a concise SoF table with a detailed evidence profile showing the judgments; symbols/colour codes were user-tested as superior to numbers for conveying strength (Akl et al. 2007). The Stiglitz–Sen–Fitoussi Commission (2009) reinforces the underlying point: the problem with composite indices is "rather that their normative implications are seldom made explicit or justified."

**Proposed robustness-profile design:**
- **Keep the factors disaggregated as a vector**, never summed. Recommended dimensions (each rated on a small ordinal scale, with a parallel certainty/quality tag à la GRADE): (1) cross-tradition recurrence, *independence-weighted* (with the independence basis attached, per Phase E); (2) source diversity; (3) temporal spread / longevity; (4) claim type (empirical/normative/prudential/metaphysical/observational — kept strictly distinct); (5) empirical support (GRADE-style certainty, see Phase G); (6) degree of contestation (presence of CONTRADICTS/IN_TENSION_WITH edges); (7) attribution integrity (from attribution_confidence); (8) survives-scrutiny (human-flagged known refutations).
- **Presentation:** a GRADE-style profile table (one row per dimension: rating + certainty + one-line basis), optionally rendered as a radar chart for at-a-glance shape comparison — but with explicit caveats: radar-chart enclosed *area* is misleading (grows with the square of values, depends on axis order), so do NOT use area as a proxy score; fix a sensible scale; always pair with the exact-value table. Cap dimensions at ~7–8.
- **Partial order, not ranking:** where users want "which insights are most robust," offer a Hasse-diagram partial order (insight A dominates B only if it is at least as robust on every dimension) rather than a sorted list. Incomparable insights stay incomparable.
- **Sensitivity analysis:** run Saltelli-style uncertainty/sensitivity analysis on any derived ordering or weighting — vary the independence-weighting, the dimension scales, and the recurrence threshold, and report how stable the conclusions are. If an ordering is not robust to defensible choices, say so.

### Phase G — Honest Use of Empirical Evidence

- **Never say "science proves the ancients right."** Tag empirical support with a GRADE-style certainty level per claim, and record the supporting study/studies with their own caveats.
- **The replication crisis and effect-size shrinkage — worked example (marshmallow test):** Watts, Duncan & Quan (2018, *Psychological Science* 29(7):1159–1177, "Revisiting the Marshmallow Test") conceptually replicated Shoda, Mischel & Peake (1990). In their words: "an additional minute waited at age 4 predicted a gain of approximately one tenth of a standard deviation in achievement at age 15. But this bivariate correlation was only half the size of those reported in the original studies and was reduced by two thirds in the presence of controls for family background, early cognitive ability, and the home environment. Most of the variation in adolescent achievement came from being able to wait at least 20 s." (The interpretation was contested — Falk, Kosse & Pinger 2020 argued censoring biased correlations toward zero; Watts & Duncan replied.) Lesson for the project: a vivid prudential maxim ("delay gratification") may have a real but much smaller and more conditional empirical basis than folklore implies — exactly the "tendencies not destinies" framing.
- **WEIRD-sample problem:** Henrich, Heine & Norenzayan (2010, *Behavioral and Brain Sciences* 33(2-3):61–83) showed psychology's evidence base is overwhelmingly Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic — and that "WEIRD subjects are particularly unusual compared with the rest of the species – frequent outliers." A decade-on editorial (Apicella, Norenzayan & Henrich 2020) found "the needle hasn't moved." So any empirical-support tag must record the sample population and flag WEIRD-only evidence as low external validity for a cross-cultural wisdom claim.
- **Publication bias & p-hacking / researcher degrees of freedom:** prefer meta-analyses, registered reports, and large replication projects over single splashy studies; tag single-study support as weak.

### Phase H — Graph Modelling & Storage

- **Single source of truth:** a property graph is the right model. **Neo4j** (labeled property graph, Cypher, MERGE-based idempotent ingestion) is the mature choice [Smarason](https://www.smarason.is/en/blog/building-institutional-knowledge-graph-neo4j) and is widely used for provenance graphs; the FAIR/Knowledge-Graph-Building-Blocks prototype and the Open Research Knowledge Graph both run on Neo4j with provenance + versioning. **A portable JSON/SQLite + embedding-index** approach is a legitimate lighter-weight alternative that maximises portability and reproducibility (everything in version-controllable text files) at the cost of graph-query power. **Recommendation:** author/edit in a portable, diff-able serialization (JSON-LD or YAML per node/edge, in Git) as the canonical store, and load into Neo4j as a queryable projection — this gives both reproducibility (Git history = versioning) and graph analytics (Cypher, GDS library for Leiden/Louvain, betweenness). W3C JSON-LD + SHACL provide a standards-compliant foundation without requiring full RDF/SPARQL infrastructure.
- **Versioning:** Git on the serialized graph gives full history, diffs, branches, and blame. For in-database versioning, the FAIR KGBB work demonstrates soft-delete + edit-history + provenance tracking on Neo4j semantic units. [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2304.09029)
- **Provenance of inferred vs curated edges — model explicitly.** Every edge carries an `origin` field (inferred | curated | inferred-then-curated) — this is the explicit lesson from Six Degrees of Francis Bacon. Use:
  - **W3C PROV-O** for the activity/agent/entity chain (most widely adopted provenance model; PROV models Entities, Agents and Activities, e.g. `prov:wasGeneratedBy`).
  - **Nanopublication** structure (assertion graph + provenance graph + publication-info graph) for each wisdom unit/claim — designed exactly for "statement + where it came from + who asserted it."
  - **Wikidata's statement model** (each claim carries references and a rank: preferred/normal/deprecated) as the pattern for handling competing/uncertain claims and an attribution_confidence analogue.
  - **PAV** (Provenance, Authoring, Versioning) vocabulary for authorship/version metadata.
- **What Six Degrees of Francis Bacon actually did (and what to learn):** it data-mined the Oxford Dictionary of National Biography (the initial set was "13,309 actor nodes and over 88 million potential edges"; the public SDFB/Folger dataset contains "171419 relationships between 15824 early modern figures... separated into 109 distinct labelled groups and the relationships fall under one of 64 labelled categories"). NER (Stanford + LingPipe) hit 96.7% recall / 65.5% precision. Relationships were *inferred* via a **Poisson Graphical Lasso** fit on 100 random subsets, summed into a **confidence matrix** giving each potential edge a 0–100 "confidence" (0 = never inferred, 100 = always inferred), qualitatively binned "possible"→"certain." Validation against expert-read ODNB relationships gave ~89% precision in the 60–100 ("likely-to-certain") band. Crucially, the public site lets users **add human-curated relationships at stated confidence (e.g. 100% for "we know they met")**, and every relationship is labelled by origin (inferred vs contributed) and confidence. **Lessons:** (1) statistically infer candidate edges, then let humans curate and override; (2) attach an explicit, bin-able confidence to every edge; (3) label every edge by origin; (4) the precision/recall trade-off is a knob the user controls (a confidence slider) — the project's website should expose the same.

### Phase I — Rendering (one graph → three products)

- **Single-source-of-truth → multiple targets** is a standard content-engineering pattern: keep semantics in the graph; generate each product via a projection/transform.
- **Print book (curated traversal):** a human-curated path through the graph; render diagrams with **Graphviz** (stable, publication-quality static layouts) or **Mermaid** (text-defined diagrams, diffable, good for forks/decision trees). The print book is a *selected subgraph* + ordering.
- **Website (full navigable graph):** **D3.js** (or a graph-viz layer) for interactive exploration; expose a Six-Degrees-style confidence/origin filter (show only curated edges, or edges above confidence X) and partial-order/robustness-profile views.
- **Text-based life-sim game (dilemmas as playable forks):** **Ink** (inkle's open-source MIT-licensed narrative scripting language; exports to JSON; integrates with Unity/Unreal/Godot; "knots" are the structural unit; designed for highly branching, recombinant narrative) is the strongest fit because it is markup that exports clean JSON and supports variables/state — a Dilemma node with its forks and LEADS_TO consequences maps naturally onto Ink knots and choices. **Twine/Twee** is excellent for rapid prototyping and standalone web fiction but lacks first-class engine integration (needs Twison/JSON conversion). **Yarn Spinner** is a middle option. **Recommendation:** generate Ink (`.ink`/JSON) programmatically from Dilemma/Consequence subgraphs; prototype in Twine if needed. The graph's Dilemma → (fork) → Consequence → (LEADS_TO) structure *is* a branching-narrative graph, so the projection is close to mechanical.

### Prior art / analogous projects

- **Six Degrees of Francis Bacon** — statistical inference + human curation of a historical network; the project's explicit model for confidence + inferred/curated labelling (see Phase H).
- **Argument mapping:** **Argdown** (Markdown-like argumentation markup with tooling), the **Argument Interchange Format (AIF)** and the **Argument Web / AIFdb** (RDF ontology for argument interchange, nodes+edges typed via an Upper Ontology + Forms Ontology), **OVA/OVA+** (AIF annotation tool), **Kialo** (large-scale pro/con debate trees). Tooling like `arguebuf` converts among AIF, Argdown, Kialo, SADFace, etc. — useful precedent for the project's multi-format export.
- **Intertextuality:** Tesserae, Passim, TRACER, TextPAIR (Phase E), plus ctext.org's reuse tools for Chinese.
- **Knowledge graphs:** **Wikidata** (statement + reference + rank model), **ConceptNet** (commonsense relation graph).
- **Curated-knowledge model:** the **Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy** (expert-authored, versioned, archived quarterly editions) — a model for curation + stable versioning.
- **Mapping the Republic of Letters** (Stanford) — visualising early-modern scholarly correspondence networks; precedent for influence/contact networks and for honest treatment of gaps in the historical record.
- **Values/ethics ontologies:** **Schwartz's Theory of Basic Human Values** (ten cross-culturally recurring values in a circumplex of openness-to-change vs conservation and self-enhancement vs self-transcendence; per Schwartz 2012, "Findings from 82 countries... provide evidence for the validity of the theory across cultures." Note that Schwartz dropped a proposed 11th value, "spirituality," for lack of cross-cultural validation — a cautionary tale about assuming universality), **Moral Foundations Theory** (Haidt/Graham), and the **ValueNet** ontology that formalises both. These are candidate structuring lenses for the Virtue/Danger/Domain layer — use as *one* lens, flagged as a Western-academic theory, not as ground truth.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Foundations (do first).**
1. Adopt CTS/CITE-URN-style canonical identifiers and TEI-compatible bibliographic metadata; store license + translator per version.
2. Implement the schema below in a Git-versioned JSON-LD/YAML store + Neo4j projection.
3. Build PROV-O / nanopublication-style provenance into every node and edge from day one; add `origin` (inferred/curated) and `attribution_confidence` (default "dubious" for unverified quotes) fields.
4. Write segmentation and annotation codebooks; run a calibration round; report Krippendorff's α per dimension.

**Stage 2 — Computational structuring.**
5. Benchmark 3–4 MTEB-leading multilingual embedding models (BGE-M3, E5-Mistral, GTE-Qwen2, Qwen3-Embedding) on a hand-labelled set of project canonical claims before committing.
6. Cluster with seeded UMAP→HDBSCAN (BERTopic), cross-validated against Leiden community detection; treat agreement as evidence.
7. Use NLI (DeBERTa/RoBERTa-MNLI) + paraphrase-mining as *candidate generators only*; route every semantic/contradiction edge through human adjudication.

**Stage 3 — The hard problems (research-grade).**
8. Build the intellectual-history layer (contact, translation, chronology) and run text-reuse detection (Passim for general corpora, Tesserae for classical, ctext.org for Chinese) to find *positive* transmission evidence. Represent independence as a graded, evidence-tagged human judgment — never a computed score.
9. Build the robustness profile as a disaggregated GRADE-style vector + Hasse-diagram partial order; run Saltelli-style sensitivity analysis on any ordering; publish the sensitivity results.

**Stage 4 — Rendering.**
10. Generate the website (D3 + confidence/origin filters), print book (curated traversal, Graphviz/Mermaid diagrams), and game (programmatically-generated Ink from Dilemma subgraphs) as three projections of the one graph.

**Benchmarks that would change the plan:**
- If human-vs-LLM Krippendorff's α < ~0.67 on a dimension → keep that dimension fully human-coded.
- If embedding clustering disagrees badly across methods → the aphorism space may not have stable thematic structure; fall back to human-curated domains.
- If text-reuse detection reveals pervasive transmission among "independent" convergences → down-weight recurrence as a signal and foreground the intellectual-history caveat.

## Honesty / Guardrails Checklist

- **Misattribution / apocryphal quotes:** every quotation starts at `attribution_confidence = "dubious"` and is only promoted on verification against a cited primary edition (CTS URN + translator). Wikiquote is untrusted until a primary source is found. Flag famously misattributed maxims explicitly.
- **Survivorship / literate / canon bias:** record corpus composition (tradition, language, gender, era, source type) and surface it as a standing caveat; the digitized canon skews Western, male, literate, recent-translation. Actively seek non-Western and oral-derived sources; never present corpus frequency as global frequency.
- **Cherry-picking:** unitization is systematic (segment whole works, not favourite passages); record what was excluded and why.
- **False precision:** no single truth/robustness score; profiles stay disaggregated; confidences are coarse ordinal bins, not spurious decimals; run sensitivity analysis and report instability.
- **is/ought & naturalistic fallacy:** `claim_type` (empirical/normative/prudential/metaphysical/observational) is mandatory and enforced; the renderer never lets empirical support "promote" a normative claim; "tends to lead to" is never rendered as "should."
- **p-hacking / researcher degrees of freedom:** preregister the analysis pipeline (embedding model, clustering params, thresholds) before running; report all parameter choices; prefer meta-analytic/replicated empirical support; tag single-study and WEIRD-only evidence as weak.
- **Convergence ≠ truth, recurrence ≠ proof:** hard-coded into the schema (robustness is a separate vector from any truth claim) and into the UI copy.

## Proposed Data Schema

**Node types** (confirm existing 11; additions in *italics*): Insight, Dilemma, Virtue, Danger, Domain, LifeStage, Practice, Consequence, Thinker, Tradition, Work, *Source/Version* (a specific edition/translation with CTS-URN + license + translator, distinct from the abstract Work), *Study* (an empirical study backing an empirical claim, with its own caveats), *EvidenceClaim* (optional, to keep empirical support as first-class and gradeable).

**Edge types** (confirm existing three layers; additions in *italics*):
- Attribution: EXPRESSED_BY, FROM_WORK, IN_TRADITION, INFLUENCED, *APPEARS_IN_VERSION* (Insight→Source/Version with passage URN).
- Semantic: CONVERGES_WITH, REFINES/QUALIFIES, *split CONTRADICTS (logical) from IN_TENSION_WITH (prudential/conditional)* — recommended per Open Question 7.
- Functional: ADDRESSES, CULTIVATES, GUARDS_AGAINST, LEADS_TO, APPLIES_IN, *SUPPORTED_BY / UNDERMINED_BY (Insight↔Study/EvidenceClaim)*.

**Edge attributes (all edges):** `origin` (inferred | curated | inferred-then-curated); `confidence` (ordinal bin, Six-Degrees-style); `method` (e.g. "NLI:deberta-mnli-v1" / "human:coderID" / "passim"); `prov_activity` (PROV link); `created_version`. Convergence edges additionally carry `independence_basis` (graded, with evidence attached).

**Wisdom-unit (Insight/Dilemma) attribute schema:**
- `id` (project URN)
- `quotation` (verbatim, original language if available)
- `quotation_translation` + `translator` + `source_version_urn` (CTS-style) + `license`
- `canonical_claim` (neutral one-sentence paraphrase) + `paraphrased_by`
- `claim_type` (empirical | normative | prudential | metaphysical | observational) — **mandatory**
- `attribution_confidence` (dubious | attested | verified) — default **dubious**
- `embedding` (model+version recorded)
- `cluster_id` / `community_id` (method recorded)
- `robustness_profile` (the disaggregated vector — see Phase F — never summed)
- `prov` (PROV-O chain: source entity, extraction activity, agents)
- `version_history` (Git/soft-delete)

## Key Risks & Open Questions

1. **The independence problem is unsolved and unsolvable in the strong sense.** The project's novelty (independence-weighted convergence) rests on a judgment that can be argued but never proven. Risk: users read independence-weighting as proof of universal truth. Mitigation is epistemic framing, not better algorithms.
2. **Corpus skew is structural.** Even with active effort, the digitized public-domain canon will over-represent Western/literate/male/recent-translation sources. The graph's "recurrence" signal is partly an artifact of what got digitized.
3. **Canonical-claim paraphrasing introduces interpreter bias.** The neutral one-sentence paraphrase is where a coder's worldview enters; needs its own reliability check and is itself a normative act.
4. **NLI/embedding models encode their own (largely WEIRD, English-centric) biases**, which may systematically distort cross-cultural similarity and contradiction judgments.
5. **Versioning a graph that three products depend on** raises a release-management question: how to cut stable "editions" (like SEP's quarterly archives) so the print book cites a fixed version while the website tracks HEAD.
6. **Where does the line sit between curated editorial voice and systematic method?** The print book is curated; the more curation, the more the "map" reflects editors rather than the corpus. This tension should be made explicit to readers.
7. **Open question:** should prudential "tension" edges (advice that conflicts conditionally) be modelled as a distinct edge type from logical contradiction? The literature treats them as different; the current ontology folds them into CONTRADICTS/IN_TENSION_WITH. Recommend splitting them.
