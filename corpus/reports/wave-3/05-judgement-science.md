## A. Source-Inventory

All works below are in-copyright. Empirical-standing notes reflect the state of the literature as of mid-2026.

1. **Daniel Kahneman — *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (2011, FSG).** In-copyright. Empirical standing: MIXED-BY-CHAPTER. The heuristics-and-biases core (anchoring, availability, framing, representativeness) is largely robust; the social-priming chapter (Ch. 4) is discredited, and Kahneman himself publicly conceded in 2017 he "placed too much faith in underpowered studies." Loss aversion is real but contested in magnitude/universality.

2. **Daniel Gilbert — *Stumbling on Happiness* (2006, Knopf).** In-copyright. Empirical standing: CORE ROBUST BUT CONTESTED IN MAGNITUDE. Affective-forecasting errors (impact bias, durability bias) are a mainstream consensus but the intensity component is challenged by Levine et al. (2012) as partly a measurement artifact; the dispute is unresolved.

3. **Barry Schwartz — *The Paradox of Choice* (2004, Ecco).** In-copyright. Empirical standing: CONTESTED. The founding jam study (Iyengar & Lepper 2000) is famous but the general "choice overload" effect washed out to a mean effect size "virtually zero" in the Scheibehenne, Greifeneder & Todd (2010) meta-analysis; heavily moderated. Maximizer/satisficer scale has measurement problems.

4. **Angela Duckworth — *Grit* (2016, Scribner).** In-copyright. Empirical standing: WEAK/OVERSTATED as a large-effect construct. Credé, Tynan & Harms (2017) meta-analysis: grit correlates only modestly with performance and overlaps very strongly with conscientiousness (pooled ρ = .842 — a jangle fallacy).

5. **Martin Seligman — *Authentic Happiness* (2002), *Flourish* (2011); *Learned Optimism* (1990).** In-copyright. Empirical standing: MIXED. Some interventions (gratitude, signature-strengths) replicate at small effect sizes; PERMA is a useful framework with weak wellbeing-outcome evidence; the Losada positivity-ratio was retracted as mathematically invalid.

6. **Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi — *Flow* (1990, Harper & Row).** In-copyright. Empirical standing: WIDELY CITED, MEASUREMENT-DEBATED. Flow as a phenomenological construct is durable; operationalisation (discrete vs continuous, self-report reliance, causality) remains contested.

7. **Supporting empirical literature (journal articles, mostly not consumer-books):** Tversky & Kahneman (1974, 1981); Gilbert et al. (1998); Wilson et al. (2000); Iyengar & Lepper (2000); Brickman, Coates & Janoff-Bulman (1978); Credé et al. (2017); Scheibehenne et al. (2010); Gal & Rucker (2018); White, Uttl & Holder (2019); Brown, Sokal & Friedman (2013); Levine et al. (2012); Kahneman, Sibony & Sunstein, *Noise* (2021).

## C. Forks

**3e-F01 — Intuition vs Deliberation**
- type: fork
- canonical_claim: Fast intuition is indispensable and often accurate in well-structured, feedback-rich environments, yet it misfires predictably in novel, statistical or adversarial ones where slow deliberation is needed — the skill is knowing which situation you are in.
- addresses_dilemmas: [intuition vs deliberation]
- notes: Poles = Kahneman's System 1 (3e-001) and the biases (3e-002–007) vs Gigerenzer's "fast-and-frugal heuristics" tradition (intuition as ecologically rational). Not a winner-take-all; represent both. Convergence-to-test with Aristotelian phronesis (practical wisdom).

**3e-F02 — More Choice vs Less**
- type: fork
- canonical_claim: More options expand autonomy and the chance of a good match, yet past a point they can breed regret and paralysis — but whether "less is more" holds depends heavily on context, and the average effect across studies is near zero.
- addresses_dilemmas: [more choice vs less]
- notes: Poles = Schwartz/Iyengar-Lepper (3e-014/015) vs Scheibehenne et al. null (3e-016). The honest resolution is "it depends on identifiable moderators," not either slogan.

**3e-F03 — Grit/Effort vs Acceptance-and-Fit**
- type: fork
- canonical_claim: Perseverance through difficulty is a genuine virtue, but so is knowing when to quit a poor fit and redirect effort — treating grit as an unconditional good ignores opportunity cost and structural constraint.
- addresses_dilemmas: [grit/effort vs acceptance-and-fit]
- notes: Poles = Duckworth's grit (3e-018) vs the wisdom of strategic quitting / person-environment fit. Convergence-to-test with Stoic "control what is yours" and Serenity-Prayer discernment.

**3e-F04 — Optimise vs Good-Enough**
- type: fork
- canonical_claim: Maximising can secure objectively better outcomes while eroding satisfaction with them; satisficing sacrifices some optimality for peace of mind and time — each is rational for different stakes.
- addresses_dilemmas: [optimise vs good-enough]
- notes: Poles = maximiser vs satisficer (3e-017). Convergence-to-test with ancient moderation/"the golden mean" and Simon's bounded rationality.

**3e-F05 — Predicting Our Future Feelings vs Distrusting Those Predictions**
- type: fork
- canonical_claim: We must forecast our feelings to make life decisions, yet we know those forecasts are biased — so wisdom lies in forecasting while discounting our confidence and consulting others' actual experience.
- addresses_dilemmas: [predicting future feelings vs distrusting those predictions]
- notes: Poles = necessity of affective forecasting vs impact bias (3e-009–013). Note this fork is itself under dispute given the Levine artifact challenge.

**3e-F06 — Cultivated Optimism vs Clear-Eyed Realism**
- type: fork
- canonical_claim: A hopeful explanatory style protects against helplessness and aids persistence, but optimism divorced from accuracy becomes self-deception; the goal is accurate, not rosy, appraisal.
- addresses_dilemmas: [cultivated optimism vs clear-eyed realism]
- notes: Poles = Seligman's learned optimism (3e-020) vs "depressive realism" and toxic-positivity critiques. Seligman himself frames the aim as accurate attribution, not blanket positivity.

## D. Virtues & Dangers

- **Cultivates:** epistemic humility (knowing our judgement is systematically fallible); equanimity and resilience (we cope better than we predict); intellectual honesty (the field's own self-correction); practical wisdom (matching decision style to stakes); contentment (satisficing, gratitude); engagement and mastery (flow); fairness/consistency (noise reduction).
- **Guards against:** overconfidence and jumping to conclusions (WYSIATI, planning fallacy); manipulation (anchoring, framing); materialism and the hedonic-treadmill trap; regret and paralysis (choice, maximising); catastrophising (immune neglect); pseudoscience and false precision (positivity ratio, social priming); victim-blaming (grit overreach); toxic positivity (optimism without realism).
- **Danger native to this literature itself:** scientism — dressing prudential or normative life-advice in borrowed empirical authority. Several load-bearing "findings" here are contested (choice overload, grit, loss-aversion magnitude, positivity ratio, social priming, strong hedonic set-point). The virtue the corpus should model is precisely the candour these fields learned the hard way.

## E. Convergences-to-Test (proposed, NOT asserted)

1. **Stoic "control what is yours" (dichotomy of control) ↔ affective-forecasting humility + immune neglect (3e-009/010):** both counsel loosening attachment to predicted external outcomes because our inner adaptation is stronger, and our forecasts weaker, than we think.
2. **Ancient moderation / Aristotelian golden mean ↔ satisficing (3e-017) and choice-restraint (3e-014):** "good enough" as an ethical and prudential stance, not merely a decision shortcut.
3. **Carpe diem / memento mori / finitude ↔ hedonic adaptation (3e-025):** if joys and sorrows both fade to baseline, attention to the present and to meaning (rather than acquisition) is rational.
4. **Buddhist non-attachment / impermanence ↔ hedonic treadmill + impact bias:** convergent diagnosis of craving and mis-forecast satisfaction; test whether the mechanisms described actually align or merely rhyme.
5. **Aristotelian phronesis / practical wisdom ↔ intuition-vs-deliberation fork (3e-F01) and Kahneman's expert-intuition conditions:** when is trained intuition trustworthy?
6. **Aristotelian eudaimonia / "activity of the soul in accordance with virtue" ↔ flow (3e-024) and PERMA's engagement/meaning (3e-021):** engagement and meaning over raw pleasure.
7. **Gratitude practices in Stoicism and religious traditions ↔ gratitude interventions (3e-022):** ancient practice, small measurable modern effect — test the mapping.

*All flagged as convergences TO TEST at clustering; recurrence across traditions is robustness/interest, not proof.*

## F. Misattribution-Watchlist

1. **Dual-system model as literal brain anatomy.** Popular versions treat "System 1" and "System 2" as two physical brain systems or little agents. Kahneman explicitly used them as expository fictions and warned against reification. FLAG.
2. **"10,000 hours" conflated with grit / Duckworth.** The 10,000-hours popularisation (Gladwell, from Ericsson's deliberate-practice work) is frequently merged with grit; Ericsson himself objected to the round-number simplification, and deliberate practice ≠ grit. Neither equals guaranteed mastery. FLAG.
3. **"We only use 10% of our brains / X% of willpower."** Folk claims about untapped willpower reserves ride on the now-contested ego-depletion literature (see report 08). No basis for a fixed willpower percentage. FLAG.
4. **The 3-to-1 positivity ratio as established science.** Still circulated in coaching/HR; the precise ratio (2.9013) was retracted as mathematically invalid (3e-023). FLAG.
5. **Jam study as proof that "less is always more."** One field experiment, not a settled law; meta-analysis finds a near-zero mean effect (3e-016). FLAG.
6. **Loss aversion as an exact "losses hurt twice as much as gains" universal constant.** The 2:1 coefficient is context-dependent, not a fixed law of mind (3e-005). FLAG.
7. **Brickman lottery study as proof winners "end up no happier / equally happy."** The winners were slightly (non-significantly) happier and the study had small samples; strong set-point reading is contested (3e-025). FLAG.
8. **Misquoted Csikszentmihalyi / flow as any pleasurable "zone."** Flow has specific conditions (challenge-skill balance, clear goals, feedback); casual use conflates it with relaxation or mere enjoyment. FLAG. (Also: his surname is routinely misspelled/mispronounced — minor.)
9. **Maslow-style "self-actualisation" claims smuggled into PERMA.** PERMA is Seligman's, distinct from Maslow's hierarchy; don't conflate.

## G. Leads for Later Waves

1. **Replication-crisis meta-science proper:** Open Science Collaboration (2015), Many Labs projects, p-hacking / garden-of-forking-paths (Simmons, Nelson & Simonsohn; Gelman), pre-registration and registered reports — the epistemic backbone for grading this whole wave.
2. **Cross-cultural / WEIRD critique of the good-life literature:** Henrich, Heine & Norenzayan (2010) "The weirdest people in the world?"; interdependent vs independent happiness; the maximiser and positivity findings that do not travel. (Note Henrich et al. explicitly bracketed subjective-wellbeing research as less WEIRD-dependent than experimental social psychology — worth probing.)
3. **Kahneman's *Noise* (2021):** noise vs bias, decision hygiene, algorithmic vs clinical judgement — a distinct thread from *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (3e-026).
4. **The positive-psychology-intervention debate:** White, Uttl & Holder (2019) vs Sin & Lyubomirsky (2009) vs Bolier et al. (2013); publication bias and small-sample inflation in the wellbeing-intervention field.
5. **Gigerenzer's ecological-rationality programme** as the principal loyal-opposition to the heuristics-and-biases framing — fast-and-frugal heuristics, "less-is-more" effects.
6. **Nudge / libertarian paternalism (Thaler & Sunstein)** and its own partial replication reckoning — the applied arm of this science.
7. **Hedonic-adaptation frontier:** Lucas, Diener and colleagues on incomplete adaptation, set-point revision, and which life circumstances leave permanent marks — deep merge candidate with report 08.
8. **Affective-forecasting resolution:** whether the Levine artifact critique or the Gilbert-Wilson defence prevails, and any modern registered replication of impact/durability bias (currently a genuine evidentiary gap).

### Closing note on method and candour

This block deliberately separates a **robust core** (anchoring, availability, framing, planning fallacy, noise, and the *phenomenology* of flow and hedonic adaptation) from a **contested or failed periphery** (social priming, loss-aversion magnitude, choice overload, grit as a distinct large-effect construct, the positivity ratio, the strong hedonic set-point, and the full-strength intensity component of impact bias). The most important wisdom this literature offers may be second-order: it is a live demonstration that even Nobel-calibre science over-claims, that popularisation inflates effect sizes, and that intellectual honesty means saying "contested" when the evidence is contested. Every causal claim about wellbeing here should be read as a *tendency under conditions*, never a destiny — and several of the field's most quotable slogans are exactly the units a careful map for mortals must footnote rather than trust.
