# The Human Story — Big History & Where We're Heading (Wave-3, Block 3a)

**Register note:** This report treats popular macro-history (Harari, Diamond, Christian) as *interpretive argument*, not settled science. Sweeping narratives are observational/interpretive; existential-risk and AI claims are prudential/speculative; causal claims about technology and wellbeing are contested. Copyright: all focal works are **in-copyright**; `original_quote` is null by default and used only where the exact wording is itself the object of interest.

## TL;DR
- The load-bearing wisdom here is one transferable idea in three registers: humans cooperate at scale through **shared fictions** (Harari's intersubjective realities — money, nations, law, religion), which are enormously powerful *and* not literally true; the practical skill is to use the story without mistaking it for the world.
- Harari and Diamond are both **contested by working historians and anthropologists** — Harari for unsourced overgeneralization (C.R. Hallpike; neuroscientist Darshana Narayanan, whose 2022 *Current Affairs* essay calls his work "riddled with errors" that "cannot be dismissed as nit-picking") and Diamond for environmental determinism (the *Questioning Collapse* volume; the Easter Island "ecocide" case substantially overturned by Hunt & Lipo and by a 2024 *Nature* ancient-genome study). Treat their big claims as arguments to think with, not facts to repeat.
- The recurring forks: the **useful story vs. the literal truth**; **progress vs. its hidden costs** (the "luxury trap"); the **individual vs. the shared fiction that binds the group**; and **adapting to change vs. anchoring in what lasts** — none resolves cleanly, and that irreducibility is itself the wisdom.

## C. Forks (Tensions to Preserve)
1. **Useful story vs. literal truth.** Intersubjective realities (bh-001, bh-003, bh-007) and meaning (bh-014) are "fictions" both indispensable and untrue; the skill is dual vision. Christian's big history (bh-024, bh-026) sits inside this fork, offering a science-based story that critics say functions as myth.
2. **Progress vs. hidden costs.** Agriculture (bh-004), luxury trap (bh-005), "wheat domesticated us" (bh-006) argue aggregate advance and individual welfare diverge. Counter-pressure: the forager evidence is contested and the "famine/plague/war solved" claim (bh-008) looks over-confident post-COVID. Hold both the caution against progress-worship and against romanticizing the past.
3. **Individual vs. shared fiction.** The glue that makes society possible (bh-001, bh-002) can subordinate or discard the person (bh-007, bh-011, bh-013).
4. **Adapting vs. anchoring.** *21 Lessons* counsels flexibility (bh-012, bh-015); *Collapse* and Christian counsel stewardship of fragile complexity (bh-020, bh-021, bh-024).
5. **Luck vs. agency in peoples' fortunes.** Diamond's geography (bh-016) minimizes agency; critics restore it (bh-018, bh-023); the 2024 test says geography matters but is "not destiny" (bh-019). Sharpest live scholarly fork.

## D. Virtues & Dangers
**Virtues cultivated:** intellectual humility (bh-007, bh-018, bh-026, bh-028); historical/cosmic humility (bh-015, bh-024); critical reading/media literacy (bh-013, bh-028); foresight and stewardship (bh-020, bh-021, bh-024, bh-025); attention/clarity (bh-012); adaptability (bh-011, bh-012, bh-015); anti-racism (bh-016).
**Dangers guarded against:** naive progress-narratives/presentism (bh-004, bh-005, bh-006); mistaking story for fact (bh-001, bh-007, bh-022); techno-utopianism/complacency (bh-008, bh-010, bh-011, bh-027); reductive scientism (bh-009, bh-026); monocausal/determinist explanation (bh-018, bh-019, bh-028); short-termism/elite capture (bh-021).
**Dangers internal to the sources:** Harari — unsourced assertion, overgeneralization, blurring interpretation and fact, reductive "humans are algorithms" metaphysics as science. Diamond — environmental determinism, erasure of agency/colonial responsibility, over-reliance on contested cases (Rapa Nui). Christian — scientism-as-myth, humans as passive vehicles.

## F. Misattribution Watchlist
1. **"Useless class" as a verdict on people.** Harari's phrase (bh-011) is "useless from the viewpoint of the economic and political system," not a judgment of human worth; popularizers strip the qualifier. Flag any use that drops it.
2. **"Famine, plague, war are solved."** *Homo Deus* (bh-008) is a hedged trend-claim about declining proportions, not victory; often quoted flatly. COVID-19 (post-dating the book) makes the flat version false.
3. **"Biggest fraud" / "wheat domesticated us"** (bh-004, bh-006) are Harari's provocations, often repeated as archaeological consensus. Interpretive; do not tag empirical.
4. **Diamond as "the geography guy who says poor countries are doomed."** Diamond himself calls this a "misunderstanding," arguing that knowing the causes of poverty is what enables change. Flag fatalist paraphrases.
5. **Easter Island "ecocide" as settled fact.** Widely circulated (including Diamond's own phrasing that Rapa Nui is the "clearest example of a society that destroyed itself") but contested/overturned (bh-022). Never present as established history.
6. **Chomsky "fanciful" quote.** Reported secondhand (a review citing Meglioli) that Chomsky called Harari's language-origins account "fanciful." Treat as an attributed characterization requiring primary verification; cite as reported, not confirmed.
7. **Generic viral "Harari quotes."** Aggregators (QuoteFancy etc.) circulate lines like "Lasting happiness comes only from serotonin, dopamine and oxytocin." Verify against the specific book/chapter before elevating any to `original_quote`. Default to null.
8. **"Organisms are algorithms" as Harari's own belief.** He frames it as current scientific "dogma" he reports and partly critiques; critics (Thiessen) argue he smuggles it in as his creed. Represent as a described ideal-type; flag ambiguity.
