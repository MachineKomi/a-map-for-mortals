# Sweat Soda & Slimes — Voice & Style Spec
### v1.0 · Voice reference · **GOLD**

*The authoritative map of **how the game's prose sounds, moment by moment.** The core instrument is fixed (§1) and specified to imitable detail in the voice cards (§9); what changes is the fingering (§2). This spec pairs with the **Writing Process** (the pipeline that produces the prose), the **Writing Sample** (the worked demonstration), and the **Scent & Taste Reference** (the science and descriptor palette). It is also the seed for the **Writing Tag/Register Bank** — the tag-keyed line banks the systems select from (Core Systems & Simulation spec §9.5).*

> **v1.0 — locked (2026-07-06).** First gold version; the writing layer's **voice authority** (the Writing Process wires it into the pipeline). Carries: the fixed instrument and equations (§1); the voice-by-moment map (§2); the domination braid (§3); the titles rule (§4); the orthography rules (§5); the confirmed lexicon deltas (§6); the immutables (§7); the exemplar bank — author-certified anchors plus the cast at working temperature, synthesized from the Scent & Taste Reference (§8); and the seeded voice cards for all six bench sources (§9), each imitable from the card alone.

## 1. The instrument (the core voice, fixed)

One narrator, one instrument, the whole game. The core voice is the locked bench — Mitchell-comedian dryness, "Slimes 300" serenity, Milne's supporting-cast dignity, Harris/Holland ballast, Slayers banter rhythm, *Roman Holiday* arc — **specified to imitable detail in the voice cards, §9**, with the locked default equations:

- **Izumi's interior** ≈ Mitchell 0.5 + Slimes-300 0.4 + Milne 0.1.
- **World atmosphere** ≈ Harris 0.5 + Holland 0.5.
- **Supporting-cast description** ≈ Milne 0.7 + Mitchell 0.3.
- **Banter** ≈ Slayers rhythm in the project's own diction.

These are confirmed, not revised. The scent register in particular — located, conditional, precise, human — is the strongest thing the project owns and **does not move**.

What this spec adds is the rule the defaults were missing: **the voice modulates by moment, not by document.** A scene is not "a Milne scene" or "a chronicler scene"; it is the house voice, leaning where the moment demands, and returning. The leans are bounded — a paragraph, a beat, a line — and the return to the core voice is part of the effect.

## 2. The moment map

The core artifact. For each kind of moment: the lean, the trigger, and the sound. (Calibration lines for every row: §8. How each lean's source actually works: §9.)

| Moment | Lean | The sound |
|---|---|---|
| **Izumi's interior; comedy; banter; barks** | **None — core voice.** | Dry, pedantic, self-aware, parenthetical; the aggrieved-but-reasonable rhythm; sincerity sneaking up. |
| **Scent narration, everywhere** | **None — core voice at its most precise.** | Located, causal, sensory, human; the register wall (§7) intact; precision as the courtesy the smell deserves. |
| **Onboarding, arrival, and the lightest copy** (first minutes, tutorial-adjacent scenes, soda/card item text) | **Slow-life LN: directness.** | Breezy, literal, earnest; short declaratives; a diegetic wink at game logic; "Ten out of ten. No notes." energy. Wears off as the world deepens — the opening owns it, floor eighteen doesn't. |
| **Izumi's confession beats** (desire admitted, submission surfacing — one beat at a time) | **LN plainness.** | The armour comes off for exactly one line or two — first person, flat, unhedged ("And the confession is: I didn't mind.") — then the dryness re-arms. The plainness *is* the masochism surfacing; never sustained long enough to become earnest wallpaper. |
| **Institutional, power, and deep-time establishing** (Victoria's world, the Church, processions, arrivals at old places, the villain-cult's paperwork) | **Chronicler ballast (Harris×Holland forward).** | Grave, lived, administrative; institutions treated as real and old; wit gone dry and durable. For **power and old stone only** — never the cosy village, which stays in the core voice. |
| **Ensemble hub comedy** (Vending-chan, village meddling, found-family scenes) | **Milne cadence — shapes only.** | The kindly build-and-turn sentence, the granted dignity, the repetition-with-variation ("What charts." / "No charts." / "…a great many charts."). **Never Milne's orthography** (§5). |
| **Cosy village world** (Puteolae register: places, neighbours, routine) | **None — core voice.** | The hub introduction register: affectionate, specific, unhurried. |
| **Combat log** | **Plain sensory verbs.** | Short. *Thickens, intensifies, grows more potent, ripens.* One flavour clause, no essay. |
| **Hound Statue readouts** | **The one chemistry register** — unchanged. | Dry, technical, faintly absurd edutainment; the only place compounds and microbes are named. |

**Two standing rules of the map:**

1. **The lean is a seasoning, not a key change.** If a whole page sounds like the lean, the pass has failed; the core voice must be audible underneath and must get the closer.
2. **When two drafts tie, take the shorter, plainer one.** Direct and literal beats decorated, everywhere.

## 3. The domination register (the braid)

The register for the warm-domination material — Victoria's audiences above all, and every scene where a dominant woman has Izumi where she wants him — is a **braid of three voices**, switched by what the beat is doing:

1. **Her presence, her world, her rituals → chronicler gravity.** The house, the silence, the ceremony of her attention. She is written the way an institution is written: old, real, and unimpressed. Fear of her is a form of appreciation, and the prose is allowed to feel it. *"A great house publishes its Mistress before you reach her."*
2. **His reaction → LN confession.** When the submission surfaces, Izumi goes plain for a beat — no jokes, no parentheses — and then recovers his armour. The gap between the two registers is the character.
3. **The scent itself → core-voice precision, unchanged.** The one overpoweringly cheesy locus (Victoria's feet) is carried by prose that never is.

**Content rules of the register (from the Creative DNA §1/§6 — the de-sanitized shape):**

- **The surface runs the full bully spectrum:** callous cruelty, humiliation, teasing, mock-contempt, gleeful torment. She *enjoys* his shame, and the prose says so. Softening this surface is a documented failure mode, not a courtesy.
- **The floor is unchanged and absolute:** emotional, romantic, consensual, equal underneath; never physical harm; never contempt-without-care. The tell that it is love is that she keeps choosing him.
- **Never childlike.** Her delight is a cruel smirk thrilling in the torment and humiliation of its object — never the expression of a child, never immature, never self-deprecating.
- **Menace is subtle.** The dare is quiet — *"Is something wrong?"* — not announced. The most frightening version of her is the most composed one.
- **The braid is a house register, tuned per woman.** Victoria plays it as ceremony (chronicler-forward). **Masha plays it as sport** — gyaru bully weather: physical, grinning, escalation-shaped, the Slayers volley carrying the tease; her scent is a thing she *aims* (§8.2). **Lyra plays it inverted** — she is not performing dominance, she is failing to hide an appetite; the register surfaces as slips, over-corrections, and a wicked grin half disguised, wrapped in genuine wholesomeness (§8.2).

**Attribution verbs carry register.** When the line is a tease, the tag says so: *she teases*, *she rules*, *she decides* — not a flat *she says*. (Sparingly; the verb is seasoning too.)

## 4. Titles

Chapter and scene titles are **whole sentences in the slow-life LN tradition** — first person where natural, comic, slightly too informative:

> *So I Died of Overwork, Got Hit by Two Trucks, and Honestly Things Are Looking Up.*
> *The Terrifying Noble Lady Summoned Me, and I Have a Confession.*

For cosy hub ensemble pieces, the Milne form is sanctioned as the alternate:

> *In Which Vending-chan Has Charts.*

Short noun-phrase titles ("The Basket") are retired for scenes; they remain fine for menus, locations, and system surfaces.

## 5. Orthography & typography rules

1. **No decorative capitalisation.** Capital letters never mark a word as Significant or Important in narration or dialogue. (The Milne lean imports sentence shapes, never this.) *Lintable: flag any mid-sentence capitalised common noun.*
2. **Sanctioned capitals:** proper nouns; named stats/statuses where the game names them (Damp, Dehydrated); **"Mistress"** as Victoria's title/address — a deliberate exception, in-register; diegetic signage and notice-board headings, which may be full caps as objects in the world (*SANCTUARY — PLEASE DO NOT FEED THE SLIMES…*).
3. **Italics** carry stress, titles of in-world works, and interior emphasis — sparingly; an italic per paragraph is a lot.
4. **Chart/document titles inside prose** are italicised, not capitalised (*Laughter Incidence by Party Composition, Annotated*).
5. The LN lean's typographic charms (the ♪ in a bark, an exclamation in item text) are sanctioned **in short-form surfaces only** (barks, item text, popups), never in narration.

## 6. Word choices (the pleasing lexicon and its deltas)

The authority is the **Scent & Taste Reference**: §8 (descriptor palette), §8.5 (pleasing trigger words — nothing sensory is banned), §6 (the translation table), §7 (clichés). This spec adds the confirmed author-preference deltas:

- **Confirmed high-value words** (deploy with confidence, still sparingly): *ripe, ripened, ripens* · *close* · *thickens* · *intensifies* · *grows more potent* · *frank* · *moreish* · *reek(s)* (dialogue). A single well-placed one lands harder than a paragraph of them.
- **Scent-verb family for logs and running states:** *thickens, ripens, intensifies, grows more potent* — the combat log and status surfaces rotate these rather than inventing new metaphors.
- **Directness beats decoration** in short-form copy: "more direct and literal" is the author's stated preference for item text. One honest clause, then stop.
- **Attribution verbs** per §3 (*teases* over *says* when the line is a tease).
- **Diegetic plausibility of jokes:** a data/chart gag must track evidence that could actually mean what it claims (laughs per party composition: yes; clean shirts as romance evidence: no).
- **Named-status vocabulary is locked elsewhere** — *Damp* (never "Wet"), time-of-day never "energy", "Soda Loop" never said in-game (Core Systems & Simulation spec).

## 7. The immutables (what this spec may never override)

Restated so no rewrite can drift off-spec; the named gold files are the authorities.

1. **The register wall** (Scent & Taste Reference §9): narration is pure-sensory; named chemistry and microbes live only in Hound Statue blocks; no cartoon stink-clouds in narration; dialogue may go blunt, in character, sparingly.
2. **One overpoweringly cheesy locus: Victoria's feet** — an in-world fact, written well. Cheese anywhere else is a defect.
3. **The human-odour rule:** the main cast smells of human bodies — skin, salt, warmth, sweat, the locked foot phenotypes. Florals, seasonings, and desserts never as body notes; products only as products, located on the product.
4. **T-rated, always.** Heavy fan service welcome, never explicit; character-expressing, never objectifying; all romanceable characters adults.
5. **Warmth is the floor.** The de-sanitized surface (§3) sits on an unchanged floor: consensual, equal, warm underneath; no grimdark, no cruelty-without-wit, no irreversible despair.
6. **Monogamy of commitment; all routes valid** — clean-and-fresh through stink-max are all fully playable and rewarded; the writing never punishes a playstyle (Core Systems & Simulation spec §4/§9.6).
7. **Never "Roman" in-world;** Rome-inspired texture carries the flavour without the word.
8. **Minimal in-world meta:** no book/novel self-reference in narration; a little meta humour, sparingly, in character.
9. **System-driven, not LLM-in-the-loop:** shipped prose is authored, tag-keyed, and selected by the resolver (Core Systems & Simulation spec §9.5) — never generated live.
10. **The body-type aesthetic:** fertility-goddess default, one deliberate lithe exception; bodies written as power and character, never exposure.

## 8. Exemplar bank (calibration lines)

Two kinds of calibration live here. **§8.1** carries the author-certified anchors — lines the author has personally marked as the target sound. **§8.2** is the synthesis — exemplars built from the **Scent & Taste Reference** (the §6 translation table, the §4.5 disgust-register bank, the §8 palette, the §8.5 pleasing words) and the locked cast profiles, showing each love interest's scent-register at working temperature. *(The canonical per-character line banks belong to Cast & Romance and the Writing Tag/Register Bank; these are voice calibration, and the situations are illustrative.)*

### 8.1 Author-certified anchors

**Core interior:**
> The first thing to say about the day I died is that I was ahead on my deliverables.
> …the egg sandwich was the bright spot on the horizon, and I had rationed it.

**Ensemble comedy, Milne shapes without Milne dress:**
> "What charts." / "No charts." / "There are," says Vending-chan, "a great many charts." / "Am I in them." / "Madam — you are a chart all of your own."

**LN directness (arrival / light copy / titles):**
> *So I Died of Overwork, Got Hit by Two Trucks, and Honestly Things Are Looking Up.*
> **Bog-Standard Blue** — The house drink. Blue, cold, and not curious about you. Restores basically all your HP and zero per cent of your dignity.

**LN confession (the one-beat armour drop):**
> And the confession is: I didn't mind. She watched me not mind. She's very good at watching.

**Chronicler ballast (power, old stone, her):**
> A great house publishes its Mistress before you reach her.
> Honesty is the only tribute she accepts, and so it must be paid.

**Scent precision (core voice at full attention):**
> …close, dense, ripened, the smell of a stockinged foot shut in good leather since dawn through fourteen hours of being right about everything — and at its far edge a sharpness for which the only honest word is *cheese*. […] It crosses the room the way she does: without apology, expecting to be accommodated — and somehow it is the room that ends up embarrassed, and not her.

**Combat log / the acceptance dial:**
> Masha's musk thickens. The chargers come on angrier; the skittish thing at the back files a complaint and leaves.
> "It comes back," she tells the can — half a threat, half a comfort. "Give it a week." / "I know," I say.

### 8.2 The cast at working temperature (synthesized exemplars)

#### Masha — the bully who turns out soft
*Gyaru bully; strong body odour worn without apology; the corn-chip/warm-popcorn foot phenotype. Home registers: performed-teasing · bratty · knowing-wink. Her scent is not something that happens to her — it is something she **aims**. Grounding: Scent ref §6 (underarm · heavy exertion; skin · mid-day; feet · corn-chip phenotype, warm + occluded), §8 (warm-popcorn palette; travel words), §4.5 (performed/teasing disgust — "the disgust is a bit, and both parties know it").*

**Narration — her presence, aimed:**
> She drops onto the bench beside me closer than the bench requires, radiating the day like a wall the sun has spent the afternoon on — hot salt, worked leather, and under it the low animal note that is nobody's business but hers, and, by the way she is grinning, mine.

**Narration — the boots, day five (the §6 corn-chip row at full ripeness):**
> The boot comes off and the whole campfire gets the news at once: toasted, round, buttered-warm, five days deep in the leather and proud of every one of them. She watches me get it. She has arranged to watch me get it.

**Dialogue — bratty (the "smell it" command, kept warm and funny):**
> She hooks the boot off by the heel and holds it out between us like a court exhibit. "Day five. Guess." / "I'd rather—" / "*With feeling.*"

**Dialogue — performed-teasing:**
> "You're downwind," she observes. She makes no move to fix it. Neither, we both notice, do I.

**Dialogue — knowing-wink (the running joke turning; from the Scent ref §4.5 register bank):**
> "You didn't have to look that *happy* about doing my laundry."

**The floor under all of it:** the half-second backward glance that would deny everything. The tease is how she checks he's still there.

#### Lyra — the sweet one who turns out dangerous
*Shy, wholesome-presenting; the quietest body in the party — soap and clean wool until effort or fright raises warm skin and a faint clean salt; vinegar-bite boots that mortify her; and underneath, on the evenings she has enjoyed herself, the metallic tell. Her register is **shame, twice over**: honestly ashamed of her smell, and using that honest shame as cover for the appetite she is far more ashamed of. Home registers: genuine-embarrassed · deflection · the slip. Grounding: Scent ref §6 (feet · cheese-vinegar chord "sharp and frank"; skin · mid-day; intimate · metallic row as the tell's palette), §4.5 (genuine recoil played straight), §2.6 (her low, slow baseline); the tell keys off her **resolved** state only (Core Systems & Simulation spec §9.5).*

**Narration — the smell-shame (small, and the loudest thing about her):**
> Her boots come off with their quick vinegar bite — one sharp sour syllable, gone in a breath — and she folds in on herself as if the room had just been read something private aloud. It is the loudest thing about her, and it is not loud.

**Dialogue — genuine embarrassment (played straight, never a bit):**
> "I'll put them outside. It's no trouble. They like the air." *(The boots do not like the air. The boots are boots.)*

**Narration — the double shame, adjacent but never equated:**
> She apologises for her boots the way she apologises for everything — completely, structurally, with restitution offered — and I have started noticing that the apology for the boots comes easier than most. It has had the practice.

**Dialogue — the cover-up (cruelty-shame surfacing as over-correction; plant, never billboard):**
> "I wasn't smiling." A beat. "It was a grimace. Of concentration."

**Dialogue — the slip (fires only off a fight she relished):**
> "I made sure it didn't suffer. Much. — At all. I made sure it didn't suffer at all."

**Izumi's side of the plant:**
> I add it to the count I am pretending not to keep.

#### Victoria — the Mistress of the register
*Openly cruel, unashamed, exact; the most intense body odour in the cast, the sanctioned cheese locus, the occasional unspoken warm-amine edge. Home registers: mean-dommy ceremony · humiliation-pleasure · the seal. Grounding: Scent ref §6 (feet · cheese phenotype; underarm · heavy exertion), §4.5 (the two-target asymmetry — disgust at the smell **and** delight at him for liking it), §8.5 (tier-2 intensifiers in narration).*

**Dialogue — humiliation-pleasure (she collects his shame):**
> "Stand where you are. If it becomes too much for you, say so — I collect that sort of admission."

**Narration — the ceremony of it:**
> She does not hurry. Hurry is for people who are not sure the room will wait.

**Dialogue — the seal:**
> "Good boy." Two words, set down like a seal on something binding.

**Micro (mean-dommy, exact):**
> "If you have an observation about my boots, you may submit it in writing. I will grade it."

#### Izumi — the willing instrument
*The reactions the registers above play against. Home moves: precision-as-composure, the confession beat, the treacherous fondness.*

> Somewhere in the last month, *ripe* has stopped being a warning and started being a forecast. I have elected not to examine the change.
> I don't feel comfortable enough with her to mention it. I think it very loudly.

## 9. The bench voice cards (how to actually sound like the sources)

*One card per source, in the Writing Process §8.1 card format: syntax tics · rhythm · the one thing only they do · what they never do · an in-project demonstration · take/leave. These cards are the working reference — a drafter should be able to write in each lean from the card alone, without prior knowledge of the source. (When deeper calibration is needed, the Writing Process §8.1 Voice Analyst rebuilds a card from source passages; these seeds stand until then.)*

### 9.1 David Mitchell (the comedian) — Izumi's interior

- **Syntax tics:** (1) *Precision as comedy* — legal, bureaucratic, or managerial diction applied to trivial or absurd stakes ("I want that on the record"; "professionally impressed on the driver's behalf"). (2) *The scrupulous concession* — sentences that pause to be fair to an opponent nobody asked him to be fair to ("In fairness to her, and I have had cause since to be fair to her at length…"). (3) *Load-bearing parentheses* — the aside carries the real feeling while the sentence pretends to carry information.
- **Rhythm:** builds an aggrieved case over two or three lengthening clauses, then rules on it in a short flat sentence ("I have not."). Escalation is always by logic, never by volume.
- **The one thing only he does:** the aggrieved-but-reasonable register — outrage expressed as fastidious reasonableness. He is never angrier than when he is being most fair, and never funnier than when he is technically correct about something that does not matter.
- **Never does:** exclamation marks; whimsy ungoverned by logic; self-pity played straight (self-pity is admissible only as evidence in a case he is building); winning.
- **In-project demonstration:**
  > I am not saying the slime cheated at cards. I am saying it has no pockets, the ace was never recovered, and it has been avoiding me at breakfast — and a reasonable man is permitted to assemble these facts.
- **Take / leave:** take the interior monologue, the complaint-as-aria, the dignity in defeat. Leave the contemporary references, and any sourness that stays sour — ours always resolves warm.

### 9.2 "Slimes 300" (Kisetsu Morita) — the serenity, the openings, the confessions

- **Syntax tics:** (1) Short declaratives, spoken cadence, direct reader address ("I should explain—"). (2) The flat verdict line ("Ten out of ten. No notes."). (3) Parenthetical afterthoughts as their own beat ("(Spoiler: it was.)"). (4) Game logic spoken plainly as world-fact, without a wink at the fourth wall — stats and respawns are just how the world works.
- **Rhythm:** one-line paragraphs as landing pads; a confession given exactly one plain line before the deflection returns; scenes end early, on the small good thing, rather than late on the summary.
- **The one thing only it does:** *burnout gratitude* — treating rest, warmth, and having nothing to do as events worth narrating. The peace is the plot; the smallness of the pleasure is the whole point.
- **Never does:** dread; cynicism; dense description; a metaphor where a plain sentence would do; a paragraph that outstays its welcome.
- **In-project demonstration:**
  > Today I fed the slime, learned that the harbourmaster's cat has a job, and drank something blue on a warm wall. In my old department this would have been three separate performance concerns. Here it is called Tuesday.
- **Take / leave:** take the arrival relief, the sentence-titles, the one-beat confessions, the lightest copy. Leave the ♪/♥ in narration (short-form surfaces only), the exclamation density, and the tweeness that thins the world.

### 9.3 A.A. Milne — the ensemble, the village's small persons

- **Syntax tics:** (1) *The build-and-turn sentence* — a gentle premise, a scrupulous qualification, then a turn that lands on kindness or on a small true thing ("…said Izumi, to nobody, which is the safest person to say things to"). (2) *Solemn logic on small terms* — a character's or object's reasoning explained as if perfectly sound, because on its own terms it is. (3) *Repetition-with-variation* in dialogue volleys ("What charts." / "No charts." / "…a great many charts.").
- **Rhythm:** rounded, unhurried periods that resolve softly; dialogue in short flat turns; great solemnity spent on small stakes, and meaning it.
- **The one thing only he does:** *dignity granted downward* — the smallest creature's inner life honoured without a flicker of mockery. Eeyore's gloom is a philosophy, not a bit; the Guardian Pot's vigilance is a career.
- **Never does:** irony at a character's expense; menace; adult knowingness; and — in this project, by locked rule — his Capitalised Significant Words. Take the shapes, never the dress (§5).
- **In-project demonstration:**
  > The Guardian Pot had been guarding the wrong thing for some years now, but it guarded the wrong thing so faithfully that nobody had the heart to reassign it; and the wrong thing, it must be said, had never once been stolen.
- **Take / leave:** take the ensemble hub comedy, the village's small persons (pots, slimes, bats, the harbourmaster's cat), the supporting cast's interiority. Leave the capitals and the nursery frame.

### 9.4 Robert Harris × Tom Holland — power, old stone, deep time

- **Syntax tics:** (1) *Institutions and objects given agency* ("the fountain confines itself"; "A great house publishes its Mistress"). (2) *The appositive dossier* — a name, then its credentials packed between dashes ("Marcella — one year retired from adventuring, and entertaining strong views about being interrupted mid-service —"). (3) *Administrative nouns as atmosphere* — ledger, chit, tithe, censer, seal, record; awe carried by procedure, not adjectives. (4) *The judicial aside* ("which is the local way of saying that empires come and go but the pier must hold").
- **Rhythm:** long, balanced periods that survey — establish, particularise, deliver a verdict; the sting arrives in the final clause, dry and unhurried.
- **The one thing only they do:** *awe via administration* — deep time and power made real through infrastructure, paperwork, and the things that outlast their builders. Menace, when wanted, arrives as orderliness.
- **Never does:** gush; epic-fantasy diction; "in a world where"; and — locked rule — the word "Roman" in-world. The texture carries it.
- **In-project demonstration:**
  > The tithe-collectors' smiles were of the kind issued rather than felt, and the village, which had outlasted three empires and at least one theology, returned them at the exchange rate it judged fair.
- **Take / leave:** take Victoria's world, the Church, Landfall, the villain-cult's paperwork, arrivals at old places. Leave the full-chronicle distance in intimate scenes — it muffles the first person — and the body-count gravity.

### 9.5 Slayers — the banter engine

- **Syntax tics:** (1) Short volleys, three to five lines, each escalating the last by one absurd notch. (2) *Insult-as-affection* — the mean read that is obviously a form of attention. (3) Feelings smuggled inside arguments about logistics: who packed the sodas is never about the sodas.
- **Rhythm:** the locked shape — **bicker → bicker → one quiet honest line → bicker.** The honest line is never remarked on; remarking on it is the failure. The comedy resumes as if nothing happened, because for these people the honest line *is* nothing happening.
- **The one thing only it does:** comic escalation with real feeling underneath — the fight is the friendship.
- **Never does:** banter during the emotional beat itself; a volley that stalls the scene; a jab that lands as actual cruelty.
- **In-project demonstration:**
  > "You counted the cans." / "Somebody has to." / "You *labelled* them." / "Rationing is a love language." / A pause. "Mine's the melon one," she says, and we never speak of it again.
- **Take / leave:** take all party banter and the tease-escalations (Masha's bully weather runs on this engine). Leave the anime-scale slapstick that would break the PC-98 warmth.

### 9.6 *Roman Holiday* — the arc (a shape, not a prose voice)

- **The shape:** borrowed joy → truth admitted late → a withholding or a parting that enriches instead of wounds. The biggest line in the film is the one nobody says; the ending is a press conference where two people say everything by saying nothing in particular.
- **Craft moves:** stage feeling in objects and rituals (a can, a battered paperback, a pair of boots); let the audience complete the sentence; give the honest beat once, quietly, and trust it.
- **Never does:** declared feelings where staged ones would do; a clean loss; an ending that punishes warmth. *(Project bend: our arcs land warmer than the film's — changed and enriched, and together. Bittersweet is permitted; tragedy is not.)*
- **In-project demonstration:** the onsen closer — the honest reply unearned and therefore unsaid; a cold can handed over in its place; *"I know," I say* — agreement about the laundry, heard by nobody as what it is.
- **Take / leave:** take the route arcs, the big beats, the endings, the withheld line. Leave the parting.
